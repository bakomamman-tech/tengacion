# Tengacion Ecosystem 30/60/90 Day Execution Roadmap

## Purpose

This roadmap is the sixth execution cycle after the foundation, operating-system, scale, expansion, and platform roadmaps. The earlier cycles made Tengacion usable, operable, launchable, expandable, and platformized. This cycle is about turning that platform into an ecosystem:

1. Creator services and professionalization
2. Fan community depth and network effects
3. Partner, sponsor, and channel integrations
4. Marketplace and financial operations maturity
5. Data, AI, governance, and multi-market resilience

The goal is to make Tengacion more than a set of product surfaces. Creators should be able to build businesses, fans should build durable relationships with creators, partners should plug into trusted workflows, and the team should understand the health of the whole ecosystem without losing control of money, safety, privacy, or quality.

## Starting baseline

This plan assumes the previous roadmap has produced these anchors:

- shared platform models for campaigns, offers, cohorts, partners, experiments, and decisions
- creator launch planner and offer builder
- fan relationship stages and lifecycle-aware notifications
- repeatable partner and sponsor operations
- platform operating dashboard
- contribution margin and cost proxy reporting
- governance, privacy, and audit automation
- platform-scale resilience, performance, and cost review
- Akuso grounded in platform objects, workflow states, permissions, and evals

The remaining problem is ecosystem leverage. Tengacion should help creators, fans, partners, and internal operators create compounding value through shared services and trusted integrations, not only through first-party campaigns.

## North-star outcomes for the next 90 days

By the end of this plan, Tengacion should have:

- creator service programs for business support, promotion, rights, events, and monetization growth
- fan community loops that encourage repeat participation, advocacy, and creator discovery
- partner and channel integration standards for trusted external growth
- ecosystem-level finance reporting across creator earnings, platform revenue, partner revenue, payouts, refunds, disputes, and costs
- a privacy-safe data exchange model for creators, partners, sponsors, and internal teams
- controlled APIs or export workflows for partner operations without exposing sensitive user data
- Akuso support for ecosystem workflows, including creator services, partner operations, community support, and governance review
- multi-market readiness criteria for expanding beyond the first proven communities

## Product principles

- Ecosystem value must improve creator outcomes: new services, partners, or integrations should help creators earn, grow, or operate more reliably.
- Network effects need guardrails: referrals, community loops, and partner channels must not increase spam, fraud, support load, or recommendation distrust.
- Partner access is earned: external workflows need permissions, scopes, audit logs, privacy limits, and revocation paths.
- Financial clarity is non-negotiable: creator earnings, platform revenue, partner revenue, campaign costs, payouts, refunds, and disputes must reconcile.
- Data sharing stays aggregated by default: fan-level private behavior is not exposed to creators, partners, or sponsors.
- Akuso assists the ecosystem, but does not become the authority for finance, legal, moderation, account, payout, or partner decisions.

## First 30 Days

### Theme

Define ecosystem programs and trusted integration boundaries.

### Product goals

- define creator service programs and eligibility
- define fan community loops that can grow without spam
- create partner integration standards and scoped access rules
- improve ecosystem finance reporting design
- define multi-market readiness gates and governance checks

### Deliverables

#### 1. Define creator service programs

- Create service programs for:
  - launch coaching
  - catalog quality review
  - pricing and packaging review
  - subscription growth review
  - live event planning
  - campaign readiness
  - rights and takedown readiness
  - payout and finance readiness
- Each program should define:
  - eligibility
  - creator commitment
  - support owner
  - Akuso support scope
  - required data
  - expected outcome
  - success metric
  - graduation condition
  - escalation path
- Keep paid or premium services separate from basic creator support.

### Exit criteria

- creator services are clearly defined and not just informal support
- each service has a measurable creator outcome
- Akuso can explain the service without promising unsupported benefits

#### 2. Design fan community loops

- Define community loops for:
  - invite a friend to a creator
  - supporter milestone
  - live event reminder and follow-up
  - saved content completion
  - subscription renewal recovery
  - creator club update
  - fan-to-fan campaign share
  - similar creator after purchase
- Add guardrails for:
  - notification frequency
  - referral abuse
  - spammy creator messaging
  - sensitive creator or fan categories
  - recommendation complaint spikes
  - repeated ignored prompts
- Keep fan identity and behavior private unless the user explicitly participates in a visible community feature.

### Exit criteria

- fan community growth loops are documented with trust limits
- notification and referral guardrails are part of the loop design
- creator dashboards use aggregate community movement, not private fan-level data

#### 3. Create partner integration standards

- Define integration levels:
  - manual report
  - scheduled export
  - scoped dashboard access
  - campaign collaboration
  - sponsor package
  - API candidate
- Each level should define:
  - allowed data
  - prohibited data
  - approval owner
  - creator consent requirement
  - sponsor labeling requirement
  - revocation path
  - audit event
  - renewal review
- Do not expose payment identifiers, private user behavior, identity verification data, or moderation-sensitive detail through partner access.

### Exit criteria

- partner access can be scoped before integrations expand
- partner reports and exports have privacy boundaries
- API candidates are separated from manual and dashboard workflows

#### 4. Design ecosystem finance reporting

- Define reporting by:
  - creator
  - creator service program
  - fan cohort
  - campaign
  - offer
  - partner
  - sponsor package
  - market or community
  - acquisition channel
- Include:
  - GMV
  - creator earnings
  - platform commission
  - partner revenue
  - sponsor revenue
  - payment fees
  - refunds
  - disputes
  - payout status
  - support cost proxy
  - infrastructure and model cost proxy
  - contribution margin
- Keep ledger reconciliation as the source of truth for finance-facing numbers.

### Exit criteria

- ecosystem finance reporting has a clear dimensional model
- partner and sponsor economics can be separated from creator commerce
- finance reports reconcile to ledgers before becoming operating decisions

#### 5. Define multi-market readiness gates

- Add gates for:
  - payment fit
  - payout fit
  - creator supply
  - fan demand
  - support language and hours
  - moderation capacity
  - content rights and takedown process
  - partner readiness
  - mobile and low-bandwidth performance
  - data and privacy requirements
- Add readiness states:
  - research
  - partner_seed
  - creator_seed
  - controlled_launch
  - growth
  - hold
  - exit
- Require stop conditions and review dates for every market or community.

### Exit criteria

- multi-market expansion has explicit gates
- new communities cannot bypass money, support, moderation, or rights readiness
- hold and exit decisions are normal operating states

### Day 30 exit criteria

- creator service programs are defined
- fan community loops are designed with guardrails
- partner integration standards are documented
- ecosystem finance reporting model is defined
- multi-market readiness gates are ready for use

- produce ecosystem finance and health reporting
- expand Akuso support for creator, fan, partner, finance, and governance workflows

- community loops improve repeat engagement for selected creators

## Akuso ecosystem roadmap

Akuso should help the ecosystem understand itself without becoming a hidden operator.

### Phase 1: Ecosystem grounding

- ground answers in creator services, partner integration levels, community loops, finance reporting, and readiness gates
- explain what each workflow can and cannot do
- route sensitive finance, legal, payout, moderation, account, and partner access actions to secure app flows
- cite the relevant creator, admin, partner, or support surface when possible

### Phase 2: Creator, fan, and partner assistance

- help creators understand service programs, launch options, pricing reviews, and campaign readiness
- help fans understand community features, subscriptions, reminders, and support paths
- help admins summarize partner state, consent requirements, report readiness, and renewal signals
- keep external or public text review-gated

### Phase 3: Ecosystem operations intelligence

- summarize creator service outcomes, fan community health, partner access risk, finance movement, support load, and market readiness
- flag missing data before making recommendations
- escalate conflicting metrics or risky patterns to human review
- preserve source context for audit and follow-up

### Phase 4: Eval, privacy, and cost discipline

- add evals for creator service claims, fan privacy, partner access, finance escalation, market readiness, and export refusal
- monitor helpfulness by creator, fan, support, admin, and partner workflow
- keep lightweight paths for simple explanations
- reserve heavier model use for synthesis and conflicting-signal review

## Decision rules

- If creator services do not improve creator outcomes, simplify or pause them.
- If fan community loops increase opt-outs, complaints, or abuse, reduce scope before expanding.
- If partner access cannot be scoped and revoked, do not expand access.
- If finance reporting does not reconcile to ledgers, do not use it for partner or executive decisions.
- If sponsor revenue increases recommendation distrust, pause sponsored boosts.
- If payout, refund, or dispute pressure rises, slow monetization expansion.
- If market readiness gates fail, keep the market in research, seed, or hold.
- If Akuso ecosystem evals regress, pause the affected workflow.
- If support or moderation SLAs fall behind, do not add new partner or community launches.
- If the team is overloaded, protect this order:
  - money movement and content access
  - support, moderation, and rights response
  - creator trust and earnings
  - fan privacy and community health
  - partner and sponsor access
  - market expansion
  - automation and Akuso expansion

## What not to do yet

- do not offer creator services that cannot be measured or supported
- do not expose fan-level private behavior to creators, partners, sponsors, or external channels
- do not build broad public APIs before manual, export, and dashboard workflows prove the access model
- do not expand partner access without consent, audit, privacy review, and revocation
- do not use ecosystem finance reports for decisions before ledger reconciliation is confirmed
- do not scale fan community loops that create spam, abuse, or trust complaints
- do not let Akuso make finance, legal, moderation, account, payout, partner access, or market launch decisions
