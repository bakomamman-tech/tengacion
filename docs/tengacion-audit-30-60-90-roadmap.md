## Purpose

This roadmap is the fourteenth execution cycle after the foundation, operating-system, scale, expansion, platform, ecosystem, network, intelligence, automation, orchestration, resilience, assurance, and compliance roadmaps. The earlier cycles made Tengacion usable, operable, launchable, expandable, platformized, ecosystem-ready, network-aware, intelligence-led, automation-ready, orchestration-capable, resilience-tested, assurance-backed, and compliance-mapped. This cycle is about turning compliance operations into audit-ready proof:

1. Audit scope, evidence rooms, and control testing
2. Security, privacy, data, vendor, and access audit readiness
3. Finance, payout, creator earnings, refund, dispute, and settlement audit readiness
4. Content rights, moderation, partner, API, market, and external reporting audit readiness
5. Akuso and AI governance audit readiness

The goal is not to chase a badge before the operating system can support it. The goal is to test whether Tengacion's policies, controls, evidence, owners, exceptions, remediation, and review history can withstand structured internal review and, where appropriate, external auditor, partner, investor, or regulatory due diligence.

## Starting Baseline

This plan assumes the previous roadmap has produced these anchors:

- a compliance obligation register mapped by market, user type, workflow, data category, partner type, payment flow, content type, and Akuso capability
- a policy library for privacy, data retention, security access, payments, payouts, refunds, disputes, creator earnings, fees, content rights, moderation, partner data sharing, APIs, experiments, recommendations, automation, and AI governance
- data, vendor, subprocessor, and AI capability inventories with owners, purpose, access scope, retention, deletion, review state, and offboarding paths
- compliance gates for market launches, payment and payout expansion, creator earnings statements, subscriptions, partner onboarding, API access, exports, data products, experiments, recommendations, automations, content category expansion, and Akuso capability expansion
- remediation and accepted-risk workflows with severity, owner, due date, mitigation, residual risk, compensating controls, and re-review triggers
- compliance training, acknowledgements, operating reports, and exception queues

The remaining problem is audit discipline. Tengacion has mapped obligations and controls, but the team still needs repeatable testing, evidence sampling, control owner certification, finding management, auditor-ready rooms, management responses, and closure evidence that prove the system works beyond planning.

## North-star Outcomes For The Next 90 Days

By the end of this plan, Tengacion should have:

- an audit universe that lists auditable domains, control families, owners, evidence sources, risk scores, review cadence, and readiness state
- a scoped internal audit plan for finance, privacy, data protection, security access, vendor governance, content rights, moderation, partner and API access, external reporting, recommendations, automations, and Akuso
- evidence rooms with approved sharing levels, current source links, control maps, policy references, review history, exception logs, remediation status, and owner signoff
- a control testing method with sampling rules, pass/fail criteria, reviewer independence expectations, retest rules, and closure evidence requirements
- audit findings with severity, root cause, impacted obligations, impacted users or partners, owner, due date, remediation plan, compensating controls, and verification status
- quarterly control owner certifications for critical money, data, access, rights, partner, API, market, and Akuso workflows
- readiness packets for external reviews such as partner due diligence, security assessment, privacy review, finance review, API review, AI governance review, or future formal certification work
- an Akuso audit trail that connects model routes, prompts, policy versions, source context, evals, feedback, refusals, memory behavior, incidents, and cost controls
- an executive audit report that shows which domains are ready, which are blocked, which findings are overdue, and which commitments should pause

## Product Principles

- Audit should test reality: do not accept a control because the policy says it exists.
- Evidence needs provenance: every sampled item should point to a source system, timestamp, owner, and review state.
- Independence matters: the reviewer should not be the only person responsible for operating the control being tested.
- Findings should create fixes: an audit issue should lead to remediation, risk acceptance, or a deliberate decision to pause.
- Sampling should be meaningful: test real transactions, real access grants, real exports, real incidents, real content decisions, and real Akuso outputs.
- Audit readiness should not freeze product work: gates should help the team ship with confidence, not create paperwork for low-risk changes.
- External claims need internal proof: partner, investor, creator, market, finance, privacy, security, and AI claims should trace back to tested controls.
- AI audit is part of product audit: Akuso behavior, sources, evals, prompts, refusals, memory, incidents, and model routing need reviewable evidence.

## Akuso Audit Roadmap

Akuso should be audited as an operating system component that can affect user trust, support quality, market claims, finance guidance, rights boundaries, privacy expectations, and product decisions.

### Phase 1: Audit Scope And Sampling

- define auditable Akuso domains:
  - feature guidance
  - creator workflow help
  - purchase and subscription guidance
  - payout readiness guidance
  - support triage
  - incident-mode guidance
  - creator writing
  - admin review summaries
  - partner or market summaries
- define sample sets by route, user role, workflow, risk level, model route, and source type
- identify which outputs require source evidence, eval evidence, refusal evidence, or human review
- mark unsupported high-risk domains as blocked

### Phase 2: Source, Eval, Prompt, And Policy Testing

- sample Akuso outputs and trace them to source context, feature registry entries, route hints, policy rules, eval fixtures, and model routes
- review prompt and policy changes against approval history
- test refusals for payments, payouts, account security, partner access, legal, rights, moderation, privacy, finance, and private incident boundaries
- verify eval reports are current and failures create remediation

### Phase 3: Privacy, Memory, Incident, And Cost Testing

- sample memory records for redaction, role scoping, retention, deletion, and privilege downgrade behavior
- review incident-mode answers for conservative guidance, private detail boundaries, and supported recovery claims
- verify cost and routing controls for model selection, fallback, surge conditions, and budget exceptions
- review user feedback and unresolved review items for repeated assistant control failures

### Phase 4: External-safe AI Audit Summary

- prepare external-safe summaries for:
  - Akuso scope
  - model and vendor approach
  - privacy boundaries
  - source grounding
  - eval process
  - refusal boundaries
  - human review
  - incident response
  - cost and routing controls
  - known limitations
- keep sensitive prompts, private incidents, security details, user data, and policy-sensitive analysis restricted
- ensure external AI claims match tested evidence

## Decision Rules

- If a control cannot be tested, do not use it to support external readiness.
- If evidence is stale, incomplete, disputed, replaced, or withdrawn, do not use it for audit support.
- If finance samples fail for purchase, entitlement, payout, wallet, refund, dispute, subscription, fee, or settlement controls, pause affected money movement expansion.
- If privacy, deletion, retention, access, vendor, or security samples fail, pause affected data sharing, exports, APIs, Akuso grounding, and partner expansion.
- If content rights, takedown, moderation, appeal, or restricted content samples fail, pause affected content category, market, recommendation, or campaign expansion.
- If partner, sponsor, API, export, dashboard, or external report samples fail, pause affected external access or claims.
- If Akuso samples fail on source grounding, privacy, refusal, high-risk boundaries, incident mode, or unsupported claims, pause affected assistant capability.
- If a finding is accepted as risk, it needs an owner, expiration, compensating control, and external sharing impact.
- If the team is overloaded, protect this order:
  - critical money movement, privacy, security, and access findings
  - high-risk rights, moderation, partner, API, and external reporting findings
  - Akuso high-risk boundary and privacy findings
  - market launch and partner due diligence blockers
  - lower-risk observations and documentation cleanup

## What Not To Do Yet

- do not pursue formal certification before high-risk internal controls can be tested and retested
- do not treat a policy, dashboard, or owner statement as audit evidence without source traceability
- do not share restricted evidence externally without approved classification
- do not hide failed samples inside aggregate pass rates
- do not accept unresolved critical findings without an explicit pause, compensating control, or risk decision
- do not use untested controls to support partner, investor, market, regulatory, or public claims
- do not expand Akuso into high-risk domains when source, eval, refusal, memory, or incident evidence is failing
- do not let audit become blame work; findings should improve systems, ownership, and trust
