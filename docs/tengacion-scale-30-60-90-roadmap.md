# Tengacion Scale 30/60/90 Day Execution Roadmap

Follow-on roadmap:
[Tengacion Expansion 30/60/90 Day Execution Roadmap](./tengacion-expansion-30-60-90-roadmap.md)

## Purpose

This roadmap is the third execution cycle after the foundation and next-cycle operating-system roadmaps. The earlier cycles made commerce, creator activation, discovery, analytics, payout operations, reliability, and Akuso governance usable. This cycle is about turning that operating base into a launchable, scalable creator business:

1. Controlled launch readiness
2. Creator and fan growth loops
3. Revenue expansion
4. Partnership readiness
5. Trust, governance, and resilient scale

The goal is to grow Tengacion without losing control of money movement, creator trust, recommendation quality, support load, or Akuso safety.

## Starting baseline

This plan assumes the previous cycles have produced these anchors:

- provider-backed Paystack and Stripe checkout
- reliable entitlement grants and reconciliation
- creator payout requests, review states, and finance ledger records
- production reliability snapshots and runbooks
- creator dashboard prompts, subscription analytics, and catalog quality signals
- fan return-path primitives across follows, saves, continue flows, notifications, live, and subscriptions
- recommendation diagnostics, diversity controls, and trust signals
- Akuso eval gates, review queues, route-quality targets, and bounded personalization
- executive operating dashboard and launch/governance readiness reporting

The remaining problem is not whether Tengacion can work. It is whether the team can grow usage, revenue, and partnerships while keeping the product explainable and dependable.

## North-star outcomes for the next 90 days

By the end of this plan, Tengacion should have:

- a controlled public launch path with incident, support, and rollback coverage
- a measurable creator cohort program that increases publish rate, earnings, and retention
- fan lifecycle loops that improve first-week activation, repeat visits, repeat purchases, and subscription renewal
- revenue experiments for bundles, campaigns, promoted drops, and partner opportunities
- partner-ready reporting for labels, publishers, event organizers, brands, and creator communities
- production SLOs for checkout, entitlement, payout, media, live, discovery, and Akuso
- a launch report that shows traction, unit economics, operating risks, and the next investment areas

## Product principles

- Grow in cohorts before growing broadly: expand audience only when support, payouts, and moderation can handle the next step.
- Keep creators earning: creator trust depends on visible sales, clear payouts, and responsive support.
- Every growth loop needs an owner: prompts, campaigns, notifications, and experiments should not ship without measurement and follow-up.
- Recommendations stay accountable: rank explanations, complaint signals, and diversity controls remain visible during growth.
- Revenue experiments must be reversible: discounts, bundles, campaigns, and partner offers need start and end conditions.
- Akuso can accelerate workflows, but sensitive actions stay in secure app flows with backend authorization.

## First 30 Days

### Theme

Prepare Tengacion for controlled launch.

### Product goals

- finalize launch gates and rollback plans
- choose the first creator and fan launch cohorts
- make first-week activation measurable
- prepare support, moderation, payout, and incident operations for public traffic
- define the first revenue campaigns without increasing finance risk

### Deliverables

#### 1. Create the launch readiness command center

- Build one launch checklist that covers:
  - checkout and payment callbacks
  - entitlement unlocks
  - payout request review
  - creator upload and publish flows
  - media playback and download paths
  - live session creation and joining
  - discovery fallback behavior
  - notification delivery
  - Akuso eval gates
  - support and moderation queue coverage
- Add launch gate states:
  - ready
  - watch
  - blocked
  - rollback_required
- Define rollback plans for:
  - checkout failures
  - entitlement delays
  - payout queue errors
  - media upload failures
  - notification misfires
  - recommendation complaint spikes
  - Akuso safety regressions

### Exit criteria

- launch readiness is visible in one admin-facing view or report
- each blocked gate has an owner and next action
- launch can be paused without guessing which systems are affected

#### 2. Select and prepare the first creator launch cohort

- Create cohort criteria:
  - active catalog or publish-ready content
  - clear creator category
  - completed profile
  - payout readiness
  - acceptable support and moderation risk
  - willingness to promote externally
- Add creator preparation checklist:
  - profile review
  - catalog metadata review
  - cover art and preview quality
  - pricing and subscription packaging
  - payout readiness confirmation
  - launch announcement draft
  - live or drop schedule
- Use Akuso for bounded copy support:
  - launch announcement
  - subscription benefit copy
  - content descriptions
  - fan update drafts

### Exit criteria

- first creator cohort is selected and launch-ready
- each creator has a visible launch plan
- catalog quality and payout readiness are checked before promotion

#### 3. Instrument first-week fan activation

- Define first meaningful actions:
  - follow a creator
  - save content
  - play or preview creator content
  - unlock paid content
  - subscribe to a creator
  - set a live reminder
  - join a live session
  - return through a notification
- Track activation paths by source:
  - direct invite
  - creator share
  - organic discovery
  - notification
  - referral
  - campaign
- Add first-week lifecycle states:
  - invited
  - signed_up
  - browsed
  - followed
  - saved
  - previewed
  - paid
  - subscribed
  - returned

### Exit criteria

- the team can see where new fans stall in week one
- launch cohort traffic is separated from general traffic
- first-week retention work is based on real funnel data

#### 4. Prepare reversible revenue campaigns

- Define campaign types:
  - creator drop
  - subscription launch
  - bundle offer
  - live event pass
  - marketplace creator spotlight
  - partner-sponsored feature
- Each campaign should include:
  - owner
  - start date
  - end date
  - eligible creators
  - eligible content
  - price or discount rules
  - expected margin impact
  - refund and dispute handling
  - success metric
- Keep campaign accounting traceable through the revenue ledger.

### Exit criteria

- at least one revenue campaign is ready to run without manual accounting confusion
- discounts or special offers can be disabled cleanly
- campaign impact can be separated from baseline GMV

#### 5. Harden public support and trust operations

- Add support macros for:
  - payment succeeded but content did not unlock
  - payout request pending review
  - creator verification or payout blocker
  - upload failed
  - subscription renewal failed
  - live event access issue
  - recommendation complaint
  - Akuso unsafe or incorrect answer
- Define moderation SLAs for:
  - content reports
  - marketplace disputes
  - creator profile reports
  - recommendation reports
  - assistant output reports
- Add escalation paths for high-risk account, payment, identity, and safety issues.

### Exit criteria

- support can answer launch issues consistently
- high-risk reports have a clear escalation path
- support trends feed back into product, Akuso, and roadmap reviews

### Day 30 exit criteria

- launch command center is ready
- first creator cohort is prepared
- first-week fan activation is measurable
- first revenue campaigns are reversible and ledger-aware
- support, moderation, payout, and Akuso launch operations have owners

## Days 31-60

### Theme

Turn controlled launch into repeatable growth.

### Product goals

- launch creator cohort programs
- run measured campaigns
- improve fan activation and retention from actual behavior
- package partner-facing proof
- keep incidents, complaints, and support load inside acceptable limits

### Deliverables

#### 1. Launch creator cohort programs

- Create lifecycle programs:
  - new creator activation
  - first paid drop
  - subscription launch
  - live event launch
  - dormant creator reactivation
  - high-potential creator growth
- Each program should define:
  - entry trigger
  - creator task list
  - Akuso support template
  - support owner
  - target metric
  - graduation condition
- Track:
  - prompt shown
  - prompt accepted
  - content published
  - campaign launched
  - revenue generated
  - creator retained

### Exit criteria

- creator growth work is managed through named programs
- each program produces measurable actions
- creators who stall are visible before they churn

#### 2. Run the first campaign and drop calendar

- Build a 4-week calendar covering:
  - featured creator drops
  - live sessions
  - subscription launch pushes
  - marketplace spotlights
  - fan reminders
  - editorial discovery collections
- Add campaign reporting:
  - impressions
  - clicks
  - previews
  - purchases
  - subscriptions
  - refunds
  - creator earnings
  - support contacts
  - recommendation hides or reports
- Compare campaign traffic against baseline creator performance.

### Exit criteria

- team can identify which campaign formats create repeat revenue
- campaign promotion does not hide trust or support problems
- creator-facing reports explain what changed during campaign weeks

#### 3. Improve fan lifecycle retention

- Add retention interventions for:
  - no first follow after signup
  - follow but no return
  - preview but no purchase
  - purchase but no repeat visit
  - subscription renewal risk
  - live reminder set but no join
  - saved content not resumed
- Tune notification rules by:
  - user lifecycle stage
  - creator relationship strength
  - content freshness
  - prior notification engagement
  - opt-out and complaint risk
- Keep a visible suppression reason when a notification is not sent.

### Exit criteria

- retention work is tied to lifecycle state, not one-off reminders
- notification targeting improves return behavior without raising opt-outs
- creator relationships drive repeat engagement

#### 4. Package partner-ready reporting

- Create reporting views for:
  - creator cohort performance
  - campaign impact
  - live event performance
  - subscription renewal and churn
  - fan retention by source
  - GMV, net revenue, and creator earnings
  - payout timeliness
  - support and moderation health
- Prepare partner summaries for:
  - labels
  - publishers
  - creator communities
  - venues and event organizers
  - brand sponsors
- Keep private user, payment, identity, and safety data out of partner exports.

### Exit criteria

- partnership conversations can use trusted product data
- creator and campaign performance is explainable without manual spreadsheet stitching
- external reports preserve privacy and finance boundaries

#### 5. Expand Akuso from support helper to launch copilot

- Add launch-aware Akuso coverage for:
  - creator launch checklist help
  - campaign copy drafts
  - fan support navigation
  - payout status explanations
  - subscription renewal help
  - admin incident summaries
- Add eval fixtures for:
  - campaign setup guidance
  - creator cohort help
  - support escalation quality
  - unsafe finance or account action refusal
  - partner-reporting privacy boundaries
- Keep Akuso output reviewable for public-facing copy.

### Exit criteria

- Akuso supports launch work without bypassing app controls
- launch-specific assistant failures become eval cases
- admin and support summaries reduce review time without replacing human decisions

### Day 60 exit criteria

- creator cohort programs are running
- campaign calendar and reporting are active
- fan lifecycle interventions are measurable
- partner-ready reports exist
- Akuso has launch-copilot coverage and evals

## Days 61-90

### Theme

Scale with evidence.

### Product goals

- prepare for broader public launch or partnership expansion
- formalize SLOs and risk controls
- improve performance and cost efficiency under growth
- make unit economics and operating risk visible
- decide the next investment areas from data

### Deliverables

#### 1. Define production SLOs and error budgets

- Set SLOs for:
  - checkout initialization
  - payment verification
  - entitlement grant delay
  - payout review and payout completion
  - media upload success
  - live join success
  - discovery response availability
  - notification delivery
  - Akuso response availability and eval pass rate
- Add error budgets by severity:
  - watch
  - degraded
  - incident
  - blocked
- Tie SLO breaches to:
  - owner
  - runbook
  - user impact
  - rollback criteria
  - follow-up ticket

### Exit criteria

- reliability decisions are made against defined targets
- launch expansion pauses automatically when critical budgets are exhausted
- incidents produce product and operational follow-up, not only status notes

#### 2. Optimize performance, cost, and low-bandwidth experience

- Review:
  - API latency by route
  - database query hotspots
  - media upload and playback latency
  - image and audio payload sizes
  - live session creation time
  - Akuso model cost and latency
  - notification delivery cost
- Add low-bandwidth paths for:
  - creator profile browsing
  - audio preview
  - book preview
  - saved and continue flows
  - admin operations pages
- Tune model routing so Akuso uses heavier models only when needed.

### Exit criteria

- core launch flows work on constrained mobile networks
- infrastructure and model costs are visible per growth unit
- performance regressions are caught before broad campaigns

#### 3. Create partner and sponsor pilots

- Define pilot types:
  - label or artist cohort
  - publisher or author cohort
  - live event partner
  - campus or creator-community launch
  - brand-sponsored creator collection
- Each pilot should include:
  - partner owner
  - eligible creators
  - eligible fans or geography
  - offer structure
  - reporting package
  - moderation and content-rights review
  - finance treatment
  - exit criteria
- Keep sponsored and partner surfaces clearly labeled.

### Exit criteria

- at least one partner pilot is ready to execute
- partner inventory is measurable and brand-safe
- finance, trust, and reporting requirements are clear before launch

#### 4. Formalize governance and compliance readiness

- Create review checklists for:
  - payment and payout controls
  - refund and dispute process
  - content ownership and takedown requests
  - creator verification and impersonation risk
  - privacy and data retention
  - notification consent and opt-out handling
  - recommendation complaint review
  - Akuso safety, evals, and memory boundaries
- Keep legal and regulatory interpretation with qualified counsel.
- Make every high-risk manual override auditable.

### Exit criteria

- launch expansion has documented governance boundaries
- high-risk decisions can be audited
- legal and policy gaps are visible before partner commitments

#### 5. Publish the 90-day launch and scale report

- Summarize:
  - what launched
  - cohort performance
  - creator earnings
  - GMV and net revenue
  - subscription retention
  - fan retention
  - campaign results
  - partner pipeline
  - reliability and incident history
  - support and moderation load
  - Akuso quality and eval movement
  - open risks
  - next investment areas
- Include a clear decision:
  - expand public launch
  - run another controlled cohort
  - focus on reliability and support
  - focus on creator supply
  - focus on fan retention
  - pursue partner pilots

### Exit criteria

- leadership can decide the next cycle from evidence
- growth claims are tied to operating data
- investment needs are specific and prioritized

### Day 90 exit criteria

- broader launch decision is ready
- production SLOs are defined and reviewed weekly
- performance and cost baselines are visible
- at least one partner pilot is prepared
- governance and compliance gaps are documented
- the next roadmap can choose between growth, reliability, partnerships, or monetization with evidence

## Akuso scale roadmap

Akuso should keep running in parallel with product and operations.

### Phase 1: Launch grounding

- add launch checklist and support macro grounding
- add creator cohort and campaign guidance
- keep finance, account, payout, and moderation actions behind secure app flows
- convert launch-support failures into eval fixtures

### Phase 2: Creator and campaign copilot

- provide bounded drafts for:
  - creator announcements
  - campaign copy
  - live event reminders
  - subscription renewal copy
  - partner-safe creator summaries
- require review before publishing
- track template helpfulness and edit rate

### Phase 3: Admin intelligence

- summarize incidents, support trends, payout queues, campaign results, and Akuso review backlog
- cite source metrics and admin surfaces
- avoid making operational decisions automatically
- escalate conflicting or incomplete data to human review

### Phase 4: Cost and quality optimization

- route simple support and navigation to lightweight models or deterministic responses
- reserve higher-cost models for reasoning, synthesis, and creative support
- keep eval pass rate and user helpfulness above model-cost optimization
- revisit fine-tuning only if the existing readiness gate is satisfied

## Decision rules

- If checkout, entitlement, or payout reliability is degraded, launch expansion pauses.
- If support or moderation SLAs are missed, cohort size does not grow.
- If notification opt-outs rise, lifecycle messaging slows down before adding more messages.
- If recommendation report rate rises, campaign ranking boosts pause.
- If creator earnings do not improve, creator growth programs are simplified before expanding cohorts.
- If Akuso route-quality gates regress, launch-copilot behavior pauses.
- If partner reporting cannot protect privacy or finance boundaries, partner pilots pause.
- If the team is overloaded, protect this order:
  - money movement reliability
  - content access and creator payout trust
  - launch support and moderation
  - creator cohort success
  - fan retention
  - campaigns and partnerships
  - Akuso expansion

## What not to do yet

- do not open broad public acquisition before launch gates are stable
- do not run discounts that cannot be reconciled in the revenue ledger
- do not promote creators whose payout readiness or catalog quality is unclear
- do not send aggressive notifications before opt-out and complaint metrics are trusted
- do not create partner reports that expose private user, payment, identity, or safety data
- do not let Akuso publish public copy or perform sensitive actions without human review and app authorization
- do not chase partnership scale if support, moderation, or payouts are already overloaded

## Suggested ownership split

- Product and launch: launch gates, cohort sizing, weekly priorities, campaign calendar
- Creator growth: creator cohort programs, catalog quality, creator support, Akuso templates
- Fan growth: activation funnels, lifecycle messaging, retention cohorts, notification health
- Finance and operations: campaign accounting, payouts, refunds, disputes, partner finance reports
- Backend and infrastructure: SLOs, performance, media/live reliability, rollback paths, audit logs
- Discovery and analytics: campaign attribution, recommendation trust, partner-safe reporting, executive dashboards
- AI and safety: Akuso launch copilot, eval coverage, review queues, memory and privacy boundaries
- Legal and policy: content rights, privacy, data retention, partner terms, sponsored labeling
