# Tengacion Platform 30/60/90 Day Execution Roadmap

Follow-on roadmap:
[Tengacion Ecosystem 30/60/90 Day Execution Roadmap](./tengacion-ecosystem-30-60-90-roadmap.md)

## Purpose

This roadmap is the fifth execution cycle after the foundation, operating-system, scale, and expansion roadmaps. The earlier cycles made Tengacion usable, operable, launchable, measurable, and expandable. This cycle is about turning repeated expansion work into platform capability:

1. Shared platform primitives
2. Creator business tooling
3. Fan relationship infrastructure
4. Partner and sponsor operations
5. Data intelligence, governance, and resilient scale

The goal is to make Tengacion less dependent on one-off launch work. New creator cohorts, campaigns, partner pilots, and fan growth loops should reuse common systems with clear economics, permissions, controls, and quality gates.

## Starting baseline

This plan assumes the previous roadmap has produced these anchors:

- expansion scorecards and readiness states
- self-serve creator launch playbooks
- referral and creator-share attribution
- repeatable campaign package definitions
- expansion event taxonomy and experiment metadata
- active expansion cohorts with weekly reviews
- creator monetization depth across drops, bundles, subscriptions, live, and marketplace paths
- fan referral and retention loops with notification guardrails
- low-risk operations automation with human approval for sensitive decisions
- Akuso expansion evals and grounded cohort, campaign, support, and partner-reporting coverage
- unit economics by cohort, creator category, campaign, partner, and acquisition source
- partner renewal and sponsor packages
- expansion governance checklists and decision records

The remaining problem is operational multiplication. The team should not need a new custom workflow every time Tengacion launches a creator community, campaign package, partner pilot, or monetization format.

## North-star outcomes for the next 90 days

By the end of this plan, Tengacion should have:

- platform-level models for campaigns, offers, cohorts, partners, experiments, and governance decisions
- a creator business suite that helps creators plan, launch, measure, and repeat earnings cycles
- fan relationship infrastructure that makes follows, saves, referrals, subscriptions, live, and repeat purchases work together
- partner and sponsor workflows that are reportable, privacy-safe, and finance-aware
- a trusted data layer for experiment analysis, unit economics, forecasting, and executive review
- controlled automation for repeated support, payout, moderation, campaign, and recommendation operations
- Akuso grounded as a platform copilot for creators, fans, support, admins, and partners
- reliability, privacy, and governance controls that can handle higher volume without slowing every launch to a crawl

## Product principles

- Platformize repeated behavior, not guesses: only promote workflows into platform primitives after they recur across cohorts or campaigns.
- Keep creator trust visible: earnings, payouts, catalog quality, support outcomes, and campaign performance remain first-class platform signals.
- Make fan relationships portable across surfaces: follows, saves, reminders, subscriptions, purchases, and referrals should compound instead of living in isolated features.
- Partner growth needs controls: sponsorships, reports, exports, and promoted inventory must remain labeled, auditable, and reversible.
- Data products need owners: every dashboard, cohort, experiment, and forecast should have a decision it supports.
- Automation suggests, humans approve high-risk actions: payout release, refund override, account restriction, takedown, partner export, and public AI copy remain review-gated.
- Akuso should cite platform state and guide safe action, not invent capabilities or bypass permissions.

## First 30 Days

### Theme

Convert expansion learning into platform primitives.

### Product goals

- define shared models for cohorts, campaigns, offers, partners, experiments, and decisions
- specify the creator business suite surface area
- connect fan relationship signals across discovery, notifications, subscriptions, live, and purchases
- establish data contracts for trusted experimentation and unit economics
- map governance controls to reusable platform workflows

### Deliverables

#### 1. Define the platform object model

- Create canonical definitions for:
  - cohort
  - lifecycle stage
  - campaign
  - offer
  - bundle
  - subscription package
  - live event pass
  - partner
  - sponsor package
  - experiment
  - decision record
  - governance review
- Each object should define:
  - owner
  - status
  - eligibility rules
  - start and end conditions
  - associated creators
  - associated fan cohort
  - finance treatment
  - analytics events
  - support and moderation implications
  - audit requirements

### Exit criteria

- platform objects have consistent names, states, and owners
- new workflows can reuse shared definitions instead of inventing new status models
- analytics, finance, support, and governance can reference the same objects

#### 2. Design the creator business suite

- Define creator-facing modules:
  - launch planner
  - catalog health
  - offer builder
  - campaign eligibility
  - audience and fan relationships
  - earnings and payout clarity
  - subscription renewal health
  - Akuso playbook assistant
- Define admin-facing modules:
  - creator stage
  - launch readiness
  - support risk
  - payout risk
  - campaign history
  - earnings trajectory
  - quality and moderation flags
- Keep recommendations action-oriented, measurable, and creator-safe.

### Exit criteria

- creator business suite scope is clear enough for phased implementation
- creator and admin views share the same lifecycle and readiness model
- Akuso has a defined role inside creator planning without performing sensitive actions

#### 3. Create the fan relationship model

- Define relationship signals:
  - followed creator
  - saved creator content
  - previewed content
  - completed content
  - purchased content
  - subscribed to creator
  - set live reminder
  - joined live session
  - shared creator or content
  - returned from notification
  - reported or hid recommendation
- Add relationship stages:
  - discovered
  - interested
  - engaged
  - paying
  - subscribed
  - advocate
  - dormant
  - at_risk
- Define how notifications, recommendations, and creator updates should respect these stages.

### Exit criteria

- fan retention work uses one relationship model across surfaces
- creator dashboards can show aggregate relationship movement
- notification and recommendation rules have relationship-aware guardrails

#### 4. Establish data contracts and experiment governance

- Define metric contracts for:
  - activation
  - creator earnings
  - fan retention
  - campaign conversion
  - subscription renewal
  - referral attribution
  - contribution margin
  - support load
  - recommendation trust
  - Akuso quality
- Define experiment requirements:
  - hypothesis
  - owner
  - cohort
  - variant
  - duration
  - primary metric
  - guardrail metrics
  - stop condition
  - decision date
  - result summary
- Add data quality checks before dashboards are used for executive or partner decisions.

### Exit criteria

- experiments cannot launch without a hypothesis and guardrail metrics
- metric definitions are documented and reusable
- data quality issues are visible before they become leadership narratives

#### 5. Map governance controls to platform workflows

- Create control maps for:
  - payout automation
  - refunds and disputes
  - campaign approval
  - sponsored surface approval
  - partner report export
  - content takedown
  - recommendation ranking change
  - Akuso prompt, memory, or tool change
  - user data export
- Each control should define:
  - risk level
  - approver
  - evidence required
  - audit event
  - expiration or review date
  - rollback path

### Exit criteria

- high-risk platform workflows have defined approval paths
- audit requirements are designed before automation expands
- teams know which actions can be self-serve and which require review

### Day 30 exit criteria

- platform object model is documented
- creator business suite scope is defined
- fan relationship model is documented
- experiment and metric contracts are ready
- governance controls are mapped to reusable workflows

## Akuso platform roadmap

Akuso should operate as a governed platform copilot, not a hidden decision maker.

### Phase 1: Platform grounding

- ground answers in platform objects, statuses, and permissions
- explain creator launch plans, offers, campaigns, cohorts, and partner workflows
- keep unsupported states conservative and route to secure app flows
- cite admin or creator surfaces when summarizing operational state

### Phase 2: Workflow assistance

- draft creator launch plans, offer copy, campaign updates, support responses, and partner-safe summaries
- explain why a workflow is blocked and what role can unblock it
- suggest next actions without executing sensitive changes
- require review before public, partner-facing, financial, or moderation-impacting output

### Phase 3: Operations intelligence

- summarize campaign health, payout risk, support SLA risk, moderation queue risk, experiment guardrails, and governance reviews
- identify missing data before making recommendations
- escalate conflicting signals to human review
- preserve source links and audit context

### Phase 4: Quality, cost, and safety discipline

- expand evals for platform objects, creator business suite, fan relationship stages, partner workflows, governance, and automation refusal
- route simple platform guidance to deterministic or lightweight paths
- reserve heavier reasoning for synthesis, conflicting signals, or high-context admin summaries
- keep eval pass rate and human helpfulness ahead of model cost optimization

## Decision rules

- If money movement or content access is degraded, platform expansion pauses.
- If support or moderation SLAs fall behind, campaign and partner growth slows.
- If campaign margin is weak, do not repeat the package without pricing or cost changes.
- If partner reporting requires private fan-level data, do not ship the report.
- If fan relationship messaging increases opt-outs or complaints, reduce frequency and improve targeting.
- If recommendation trust declines during sponsored or campaign boosts, pause the boost.
- If automation creates contested decisions, move the workflow back to suggestion-only mode.
- If Akuso platform evals regress, pause the affected assistant workflow.
- If data quality is unreliable, do not use the metric for executive or partner decisions.
- If the team is overloaded, protect this order:
  - checkout, entitlement, and payout reliability
  - support and moderation capacity
  - creator earnings clarity
  - fan relationship health
  - campaign and partner repeatability
  - data quality and governance
  - automation and Akuso expansion

## What not to do yet

- do not create generic platform abstractions for workflows that have not repeated
- do not make creator offers self-serve without finance, entitlement, and support guardrails
- do not expose private fan-level behavior to creators, partners, or sponsors
- do not let sponsor revenue override recommendation trust or labeling rules
- do not use dashboards for decisions before metric definitions and data quality checks exist
- do not automate payout, refund, moderation, account, partner export, or public AI decisions without human approval
- do not optimize Akuso cost by weakening safety, grounding, or eval discipline
