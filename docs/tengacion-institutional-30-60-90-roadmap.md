# Tengacion Institutional 30/60/90 Day Execution Roadmap

Follow-on roadmap:
[Tengacion Capital 30/60/90 Day Execution Roadmap](./tengacion-capital-30-60-90-roadmap.md)

## Purpose

This roadmap is the sixteenth execution cycle after the foundation, operating-system, scale, expansion, platform, ecosystem, network, intelligence, automation, orchestration, resilience, assurance, compliance, audit, and certification roadmaps. The earlier cycles made Tengacion usable, operable, launchable, expandable, platformized, ecosystem-ready, network-aware, intelligence-led, automation-ready, orchestration-capable, resilience-tested, assurance-backed, compliance-mapped, audit-ready, and certification-oriented. This cycle is about turning external trust readiness into institutional operating maturity:

1. Board-grade governance and decision records
2. Enterprise, partner, investor, and market data-room readiness
3. Financial control, capital readiness, and unit economics discipline
4. Institutional risk, security, privacy, content, and partner governance
5. Akuso AI risk governance, model operations, and external accountability

The goal is to make Tengacion able to operate with the discipline expected by serious partners, capital providers, enterprise customers, creator institutions, and market stakeholders. Institutional maturity means decisions are documented, risks are owned, controls are measured, claims are governed, and growth commitments can be defended with evidence.

## Starting Baseline

This plan assumes the previous roadmap has produced these anchors:

- a certification and external review strategy that ranks candidate paths by business value, user trust, partner demand, market pressure, risk, cost, and readiness
- trust center operations with approved public claims, restricted evidence rooms, partner packets, security and privacy summaries, finance and creator trust summaries, and AI governance summaries
- a claims register mapping external claims to owners, evidence, review dates, sharing levels, expiration, and withdrawal paths
- certification blockers and gap plans for security, privacy, data protection, access review, vendor governance, incident response, finance controls, creator earnings, partner/API access, content rights, moderation, and Akuso
- continuous evidence maintenance priorities for critical controls
- external review playbooks for assessors, partners, investors, enterprise customers, creator groups, payment providers, sponsors, and market readiness reviews
- Akuso external-readiness evidence for scope, limits, source grounding, privacy boundaries, eval process, human review, incident response, and known limitations

The remaining problem is institutional readiness. Tengacion can prepare evidence for external review, but the team still needs a stronger operating model for board-style governance, investor diligence, enterprise procurement, capital planning, executive metrics, market commitments, legal and risk decisions, and AI accountability at scale.

## North-star Outcomes For The Next 90 Days

By the end of this plan, Tengacion should have:

- an institutional governance calendar covering board-style reviews, risk reviews, finance reviews, trust reviews, market reviews, partner reviews, AI governance reviews, and post-incident executive reviews
- decision records for major product, market, partner, API, finance, security, privacy, content, recommendation, automation, and Akuso commitments
- an investor and institutional data room with approved financial, product, traction, risk, legal, compliance, security, privacy, partner, creator, and AI governance materials
- enterprise and strategic partner readiness packets that connect commercial terms, data sharing, security posture, privacy obligations, API controls, support model, reporting, and revocation paths
- finance operating discipline for unit economics, creator earnings confidence, payout exposure, refund and dispute reserves, revenue recognition support, cost controls, and capital planning
- risk appetite statements that define acceptable exposure for money movement, data sharing, market launch, partner access, content categories, recommendations, automation, and Akuso behavior
- management reporting that separates trusted metrics, disputed metrics, leading indicators, lagging indicators, commitments, risks, and unresolved exceptions
- Akuso institutional governance with AI risk appetite, capability approvals, model/vendor oversight, prompt and policy controls, eval board reporting, incident escalation, and external claim review
- a decision on whether Tengacion is ready for institutional partnerships, capital conversations, enterprise contracts, new market commitments, or another hardening cycle

## Product Principles

- Institutional trust is operational, not theatrical: every executive claim should map to current evidence, owner, and review history.
- Governance should speed good decisions: decision records, risk appetite, and owner accountability should reduce ambiguity, not create ceremony for its own sake.
- Capital readiness requires finance discipline: revenue, GMV, creator earnings, payouts, refunds, disputes, costs, and reserves should be understood before external claims.
- Enterprise readiness is cross-functional: security, privacy, support, legal, finance, API, data, incident response, and AI boundaries all matter.
- Risk appetite should be explicit: the team should know what exposure is acceptable, what needs approval, and what must pause.
- Metrics should show confidence: leadership reporting should distinguish current, stale, disputed, estimated, and unapproved numbers.
- AI accountability belongs in governance: Akuso should have the same rigor as product, data, security, and trust decisions when it affects users or external claims.
- Do not institutionalize chaos: standardize only the processes that improve trust, decisions, capital readiness, or partner confidence.

## Akuso Institutional Governance Roadmap

Akuso should be governable in board-style, partner, investor, enterprise, and market conversations without overstating what it can do.

### Phase 1: AI Risk Appetite

- define acceptable and unacceptable Akuso exposure by workflow
- define which capabilities require product, policy, legal, security, privacy, finance, or executive approval
- set warning and blocker thresholds for eval failures, unsupported claims, privacy issues, refusal failures, cost variance, latency, and incident-mode quality
- map external AI claims to risk appetite and evidence

### Phase 2: AI Governance Reporting

- report Akuso metrics in executive reviews:
  - helpfulness
  - fallback rate
  - unsupported claims
  - source coverage
  - eval pass rates
  - refusal quality
  - memory and privacy behavior
  - incident-mode quality
  - model cost
  - user feedback
- separate experimental capabilities from approved institutional claims
- show blocked capabilities and unresolved AI risks

### Phase 3: AI External Readiness

- maintain external-safe AI governance packet
- approve AI claims before investor, partner, market, or public use
- keep sensitive prompts, incidents, user data, security details, and policy-sensitive analysis restricted
- answer AI diligence questions from current source, eval, privacy, incident, and cost evidence

### Phase 4: AI Operating Accountability

- require decision records for high-risk Akuso capability changes
- tie prompt, policy, model, memory, and source changes to owner, review, eval, rollback, and incident criteria
- route repeated assistant failures to product, data, policy, or support fixes
- review Akuso as part of institutional risk, not only assistant quality

## Decision Rules

- If a decision lacks evidence, do not turn it into an external commitment.
- If a metric is disputed, estimated, or unapproved, do not use it as an institutional claim.
- If finance confidence is weak, do not pursue capital, partner, or creator earnings claims based on those numbers.
- If security, privacy, access, vendor, or incident evidence is stale, pause enterprise, partner, API, and market commitments that depend on it.
- If support, moderation, rights, or recommendation risk exceeds appetite, pause affected growth or market moves.
- If partner/API revocation and offboarding are unclear, do not expand external access.
- If Akuso evidence is stale or risk exceeds appetite, do not make external AI claims or expand affected capabilities.
- If accepted risks expire, re-review them before continuing dependent commitments.
- If the team is overloaded, protect this order:
  - finance, creator earnings, payout, refund, dispute, and capital confidence
  - privacy, security, data, vendor, and access governance
  - partner, API, market, and enterprise commitments
  - trust, safety, content rights, moderation, and support capacity
  - Akuso and AI governance
  - lower-risk data-room polish

## What Not To Do Yet

- do not use institutional language to make unsupported capital, partner, market, security, privacy, finance, or AI claims
- do not open broad diligence access before sharing classifications and claim approvals are in place
- do not present disputed or estimated metrics as trusted external numbers
- do not let data-room documents go stale while continuing to share them
- do not accept institutional risk without owner, expiration, mitigation, and re-review trigger
- do not expand enterprise, partner, API, or market commitments when revocation, support, or incident response is unclear
- do not represent Akuso as a final decision-maker for finance, legal, rights, moderation, security, partner access, market readiness, or incident status
- do not create governance ceremony that does not improve decisions, trust, or evidence quality
