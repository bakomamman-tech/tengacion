# Tengacion Expansion 30/60/90 Day Execution Roadmap

Follow-on roadmap:
[Tengacion Platform 30/60/90 Day Execution Roadmap](./tengacion-platform-30-60-90-roadmap.md)

## Purpose

This roadmap is the fourth execution cycle after the foundation, operating-system, and scale roadmaps. The earlier cycles made Tengacion usable, operable, launchable, and measurable. This cycle is about turning that launch base into a repeatable expansion machine:

1. Market and community expansion
2. Self-serve creator growth
3. Deeper monetization and partner packaging
4. Data, experimentation, and automation
5. Trust, resilience, and governance at higher volume

The goal is to grow beyond the first controlled launch cohorts while keeping unit economics, creator outcomes, support load, content quality, and Akuso behavior understandable.

## Starting baseline

This plan assumes the previous roadmap has produced these anchors:

- launch readiness gates and rollback plans
- first creator cohorts and fan activation tracking
- revenue campaign definitions and ledger-aware reporting
- public support macros, moderation SLAs, and escalation paths
- creator cohort programs and campaign/drop calendars
- fan lifecycle interventions and notification health monitoring
- partner-ready reporting and at least one partner pilot prepared
- production SLOs for checkout, entitlement, payout, media, live, discovery, notifications, and Akuso
- performance, cost, low-bandwidth, governance, and launch-scale reporting

The remaining problem is not launch readiness. It is making expansion repeatable without turning every new market, creator cohort, campaign, or partner pilot into bespoke operations work.

## North-star outcomes for the next 90 days

By the end of this plan, Tengacion should have:

- a clear expansion scorecard for markets, creator communities, and partner pilots
- self-serve creator growth paths that reduce manual onboarding pressure
- repeatable creator campaign, live event, subscription, and bundle playbooks
- fan referral and community loops that produce measurable repeat usage
- partner packages that can be sold, measured, renewed, and audited
- a data foundation for experimentation, cohort analysis, and unit economics
- safe automation around low-risk support, payout, campaign, and moderation workflows
- Akuso grounded as a creator, support, and operations copilot with eval coverage for expansion use cases

## Product principles

- Expand where the flywheel already works: do not enter a market, category, or partner channel until the current cohort shows creator earnings and fan retention.
- Make the manual loop visible before automating it: automation should remove repeated work, not hide broken judgment.
- Self-serve must still be safe: creator publishing, campaigns, payouts, and support flows need guardrails, review states, and audit logs.
- Local trust beats generic growth: pricing, payment expectations, content categories, support tone, and creator incentives should match the community being served.
- Partner revenue cannot compromise user trust: sponsored, partner, and promoted surfaces must remain labeled, measurable, and reversible.
- Akuso should reduce confusion and drafting work, not make sensitive financial, account, moderation, or legal decisions.

## First 30 Days

### Theme

Choose the next growth bets and make them repeatable.

### Product goals

- define expansion scorecards for markets, creator categories, and partners
- convert creator cohort operations into self-serve playbooks
- make referral and share attribution measurable
- turn campaign and partner pilots into reusable packages
- establish data and experiment hygiene before broader growth

### Deliverables

#### 1. Build the expansion scorecard

- Define market and community scoring inputs:
  - creator supply
  - fan demand
  - local payment fit
  - content category strength
  - expected support load
  - moderation and rights risk
  - partner access
  - acquisition cost
  - retention signal
  - payout feasibility
- Add readiness states:
  - research
  - seed
  - controlled_launch
  - expand
  - hold
  - exit
- Require every expansion bet to have:
  - owner
  - target cohort
  - launch gate status
  - expected cost
  - target metric
  - stop condition
  - review date

### Exit criteria

- the team can rank expansion bets from one shared model
- every new cohort or market has a stop condition
- growth does not proceed without payment, payout, support, and moderation readiness

#### 2. Create self-serve creator launch playbooks

- Build playbooks for:
  - first paid music drop
  - first ebook or chapter launch
  - podcast subscription launch
  - live event launch
  - creator marketplace product spotlight
  - dormant creator comeback
- Each playbook should include:
  - required profile fields
  - catalog quality checks
  - pricing guidance
  - payout readiness check
  - announcement templates
  - recommended fan actions
  - campaign eligibility rules
  - post-launch review metric
- Use Akuso for bounded drafting and checklist explanations.

### Exit criteria

- creators can prepare launches with less manual support
- support can point creators to a playbook instead of rewriting the same guidance
- Akuso answers match the real playbook steps and app capabilities

#### 3. Add referral and creator-share attribution

- Track referral sources:
  - creator profile share
  - content share
  - campaign link
  - partner link
  - fan invite
  - live event invite
- Measure:
  - invite sent
  - link opened
  - signup
  - first follow
  - first preview
  - first purchase
  - first subscription
  - D7 return
- Keep attribution privacy-safe and avoid exposing fan-level private behavior to creators or partners.

### Exit criteria

- creator-led growth can be measured from share to activation
- partner and campaign traffic can be separated from organic growth
- referral reporting protects user privacy

#### 4. Package the first repeatable campaign offers

- Define reusable campaign packages:
  - creator drop week
  - subscription starter campaign
  - live event week
  - marketplace creator spotlight
  - partner-sponsored collection
- Each package should include:
  - eligibility rules
  - promotion surfaces
  - duration
  - pricing or offer rules
  - creator obligations
  - fan messaging
  - budget or margin guardrails
  - reporting view
  - support escalation path
- Keep campaign configuration auditable and reversible.

### Exit criteria

- campaigns can be repeated without redesigning operations each time
- campaign reporting separates impressions, actions, revenue, refunds, and support load
- finance can reconcile campaign effects through existing ledgers and reports

#### 5. Establish experiment and event taxonomy hygiene

- Standardize events for:
  - activation
  - referral
  - campaign exposure
  - recommendation exposure
  - notification delivery
  - creator playbook steps
  - support escalation
  - moderation outcome
  - payout status
  - Akuso expansion guidance
- Add experiment metadata:
  - experiment key
  - variant
  - surface
  - cohort
  - start and end date
  - owner
  - success metric
  - guardrail metric
- Avoid running growth tests without guardrails for opt-outs, complaints, refunds, and support load.

### Exit criteria

- expansion data is consistent enough for weekly decisions
- experiments can be compared without manual cleanup
- guardrail metrics are reviewed beside growth metrics

### Day 30 exit criteria

- expansion scorecard is live or documented
- first self-serve creator playbooks are ready
- referral and share attribution is defined
- repeatable campaign packages exist
- experiment and event taxonomy is ready for growth work

## Days 31-60

### Theme

Run expansion loops with measurement.

### Product goals

- launch the highest-scoring expansion cohort
- run repeatable creator and partner campaigns
- improve fan referral and retention loops
- add low-risk automation for repeated operations work
- deepen Akuso coverage for expansion workflows

### Deliverables

#### 1. Launch the first expansion cohort

- Select a cohort by scorecard:
  - creator category
  - geography or community
  - partner channel
  - fan source
  - campaign package
- Track cohort health:
  - activated creators
  - published content
  - campaign participation
  - fan signups
  - paid unlocks
  - subscriptions
  - refunds
  - support contacts
  - moderation reports
  - payout readiness
- Review the cohort weekly against its stop condition.

### Exit criteria

- one expansion cohort is operating under the new scorecard
- cohort performance is visible beside launch, reliability, and finance health
- the team can decide whether to expand, hold, or exit the cohort

#### 2. Improve creator monetization depth

- Add or refine monetization paths:
  - bundles
  - limited-time drops
  - subscription tiers or benefit packages
  - live access passes
  - paid community moments
  - marketplace cross-sells
- Add creator-facing guidance:
  - who should use the offer
  - what to price
  - when to promote
  - how to evaluate results
  - what to improve next
- Keep checkout, entitlement, refund, and ledger behavior consistent across offer types.

### Exit criteria

- creators have more than one repeatable earning path
- monetization experiments do not create finance ambiguity
- creator dashboards explain which offer worked and what to try next

#### 3. Strengthen fan referral and community loops

- Add or refine fan loops:
  - invite a friend to a creator
  - share a paid drop preview
  - remind me when this creator goes live
  - continue saved content
  - renewal recovery
  - similar creator after a purchase
  - fan milestone prompts
- Track loop health:
  - source
  - conversion
  - repeat action
  - notification opt-out
  - complaint rate
  - creator benefit
- Keep fan-facing messaging frequency bounded.

### Exit criteria

- referral and community loops drive measurable repeat behavior
- notifications and prompts improve return rate without harming trust
- creators can see aggregate benefits without exposing private fan behavior

#### 4. Add low-risk operations automation

- Automate suggestions, not final decisions, for:
  - support macro selection
  - payout queue prioritization
  - campaign health warnings
  - creator playbook reminders
  - moderation queue routing
  - entitlement delay escalation
  - recommendation complaint triage
- Require human approval for:
  - payout release
  - refund override
  - account restriction
  - partner report publication
  - content takedown
  - Akuso public copy publication
- Record automation confidence, source signals, and final human action.

### Exit criteria

- repeated operations work is faster without becoming opaque
- automation mistakes are reviewable
- high-risk actions stay human-approved and auditable

#### 5. Expand Akuso for cohort, campaign, and support workflows

- Add Akuso coverage for:
  - creator playbook guidance
  - campaign package explanation
  - referral and sharing help
  - fan lifecycle support
  - support macro drafting
  - admin cohort summaries
  - partner-safe report summaries
- Add eval suites for:
  - expansion scorecard guidance
  - creator campaign setup
  - referral privacy boundaries
  - payout and refund escalation
  - partner report safety
  - unsupported automation refusal
- Track helpfulness by creator, fan, support, and admin mode.

### Exit criteria

- Akuso reduces repeated expansion support work
- expansion-specific failures are captured as eval cases
- assistant responses stay grounded in real app flows and privacy boundaries

### Day 60 exit criteria

- first expansion cohort is active and reviewed weekly
- monetization depth has improved for selected creator groups
- fan referral and retention loops are measurable
- low-risk operations automation is in place
- Akuso expansion evals and coverage are active

### Theme

Make expansion durable.

### Product goals

- decide whether to expand, hold, or exit the first expansion cohort

### Exit criteria

#### 3. Build partner renewal and sponsor packaging

- Create partner renewal package:
  - goals
  - campaign summary

### Exit criteria

- Formalize review boards or checklists for:
  - new market or community launches
  - sponsored campaigns

  - payout automation changes
  - recommendation ranking changes
  - Akuso prompt, memory, or tool changes

- Add decision records for:
  - approval
  - rejection
  - conditions
  - owner
  - expiration date

### Exit criteria

- risky changes have owners and expiration dates

#### 5. Prepare the next expansion roadmap

- Choose the next 90-day focus:
  - geographic expansion

  - partner and sponsor revenue
  - fan community depth
  - marketplace monetization
  - data platform and experimentation
  - automation and operating leverage
  - mobile and low-bandwidth growth

  - revenue potential
  - creator impact
  - fan retention impact
  - support load
  - trust and policy risk
  - engineering cost
  - time to learn

### Exit criteria

- the next cycle is chosen from expansion data
- priorities are explicit and sequenced
- the team knows what not to pursue yet

### Day 90 exit criteria

- expansion cohort decision is complete
- unit economics are visible by cohort and campaign
- partner renewal and sponsor packages are ready
- governance supports repeated launches
- next expansion focus is selected from evidence
- Akuso, automation, recommendations, and finance workflows remain reviewable

## Akuso expansion roadmap

Akuso should keep running in parallel with product, support, and operations.

### Phase 1: Playbook grounding

- ground Akuso in creator launch playbooks
- answer campaign package questions from real eligibility and setup rules
- explain referral, sharing, and privacy boundaries clearly
- keep unsupported features conservative and route users to secure app flows

### Phase 2: Cohort and campaign copilot

- draft creator announcements, campaign copy, fan updates, and partner-safe summaries
- summarize cohort health using cited admin metrics
- suggest next actions without applying them automatically
- require human review for public-facing or partner-facing text

### Phase 3: Support and operations triage

- draft support replies from approved macros
- identify likely escalation category
- summarize payout, entitlement, campaign, or moderation context
- preserve final decisions for support, finance, moderation, or admin users

### Phase 4: Expansion eval and cost discipline

- add evals for cohort guidance, campaign setup, referral privacy, support escalation, and partner reporting
- track helpfulness by mode and surface
- route simple guidance to deterministic or lighter model paths
- keep quality gates ahead of cost optimization

## Decision rules

- If checkout, entitlement, payout, or support health is degraded, expansion pauses.
- If a cohort misses activation and retention targets for two review cycles, hold or exit before adding more users.
- If creator earnings do not improve, simplify creator playbooks before adding more campaign types.
- If campaign refunds or disputes rise, pause the package and review pricing, copy, and eligibility.
- If notification opt-outs or complaints rise, reduce lifecycle messaging before adding new prompts.
- If recommendation reports rise during campaigns, pause campaign ranking boosts.
- If automation confidence is low or contested, keep the workflow manual.
- If Akuso expansion evals regress, pause affected assistant behaviors.
- If partner reporting cannot protect privacy, do not send the report.
- If the team is overloaded, protect this order:
  - money movement and access reliability
  - support and moderation capacity
  - creator earnings
  - fan retention
  - campaign repeatability
  - partner revenue
  - automation and Akuso expansion

## What not to do yet

- do not expand into new cohorts without a scorecard and stop condition
- do not automate payout, refund, moderation, or account decisions without human approval
- do not expose fan-level private behavior in creator or partner reports
- do not run campaign packages that finance cannot reconcile
- do not introduce aggressive referral incentives before fraud and abuse signals are visible
- do not let Akuso publish campaign, partner, legal, finance, or moderation decisions automatically
- do not optimize for partner revenue if creator trust or fan trust is weakening

## Suggested ownership split

- Product and expansion: scorecards, cohorts, stop conditions, roadmap decisions
- Creator growth: playbooks, creator education, campaign participation, lifecycle movement
- Fan growth: referrals, activation, retention loops, notification health
- Revenue and partnerships: campaign packages, sponsor pilots, partner renewals, reporting
- Finance and operations: ledger reconciliation, refunds, disputes, payout review, margin reporting
- Data and analytics: event taxonomy, experiments, cohort analysis, unit economics
- Backend and infrastructure: SLOs, reliability, automation plumbing, audit logs, performance
- AI and safety: Akuso grounding, evals, triage support, privacy and memory boundaries
- Trust, policy, and legal: content rights, sponsored labeling, privacy, data exports, high-risk reviews
