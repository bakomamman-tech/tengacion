# Tengacion Assurance 30/60/90 Day Execution Roadmap

Follow-on roadmap:
[Tengacion Compliance 30/60/90 Day Execution Roadmap](./tengacion-compliance-30-60-90-roadmap.md)

## Purpose

This roadmap is the twelfth execution cycle after the foundation, operating-system, scale, expansion, platform, ecosystem, network, intelligence, automation, orchestration, and resilience roadmaps. The earlier cycles made Tengacion usable, operable, launchable, expandable, platformized, ecosystem-ready, network-aware, intelligence-led, automation-ready, orchestration-capable, and resilience-tested. This cycle is about turning resilience evidence into assurance:

1. Control ownership and evidence quality
2. Finance, settlement, revenue, and payout assurance
3. Partner, API, market, and compliance readiness
4. Data, experimentation, and reporting assurance
5. Trust, safety, rights, privacy, and Akuso governance

The goal is to make Tengacion credible under review. A creator, partner, auditor, internal lead, or regulator-facing stakeholder should be able to ask why a workflow is trusted, where the evidence lives, who owns the control, how exceptions are handled, and what happens when confidence drops.

## Starting baseline

This plan assumes the previous roadmap has produced these anchors:

- critical flow SLOs, error budgets, recovery objectives, degraded modes, and resilience gates
- tested incident playbooks for checkout, entitlement, payouts, refunds, disputes, partner access, APIs, data quality, support, moderation, rights, recommendations, notifications, and Akuso regressions
- user-facing continuity states for pending payments, delayed entitlements, payout review, export delays, API degradation, campaign pauses, recommendation fallback, notification pauses, and Akuso incident mode
- standardized recovery, replay, reconciliation, correction, rollback, and post-incident workflows
- resilience economics that show the cost of incidents, degraded flows, support load, moderation load, partner failures, API abuse, data correction, Akuso cost surges, and provider outages
- resilience readiness reports that connect launch, partner, market, and workflow decisions to evidence

The remaining problem is assurance. Tengacion may know how to recover, but the team still needs stronger proof that money, access, data, partner permissions, user rights, creator trust, assistant behavior, and market commitments are controlled, reviewable, and defensible over time.

## North-star outcomes for the next 90 days

By the end of this plan, Tengacion should have:

- an assurance control registry covering money movement, entitlement, payout, refund, dispute, partner access, API access, data products, privacy, rights, moderation, recommendations, notifications, experiments, and Akuso
- evidence packs for critical workflows with owners, source systems, freshness expectations, exception status, reviewer notes, and approval history
- a monthly finance and revenue assurance close that reconciles purchases, entitlements, payouts, refunds, disputes, commissions, taxes or fees where applicable, and creator-facing balances
- partner, sponsor, API, and market readiness packets that can support due diligence without ad hoc document hunting
- data contracts and metric trust states for executive, creator, partner, finance, recommendation, experimentation, and automation metrics
- privacy, consent, rights, moderation, recommendation, and notification controls that are auditable and tied to user-facing choices
- Akuso governance with model routing controls, source coverage checks, eval evidence, incident review, cost controls, refusal quality, and high-risk workflow boundaries
- an assurance review board that can decide whether workflows, markets, partners, APIs, data products, automations, and assistant behaviors are ready to expand

## Product principles

- Assurance is evidence, not confidence: every critical claim should point to a current source, owner, control, and exception path.
- Money and access need the strongest proof: purchases, entitlements, payouts, refunds, disputes, and settlement reports must reconcile before growth claims are trusted.
- Partner trust is revocable: partner, sponsor, dashboard, export, and API access should be scoped, reviewed, paused, and withdrawn when assurance fails.
- Metrics need provenance: dashboards, experiments, recommendations, automations, and partner reports should show whether their source data is fresh, complete, blocked, corrected, or disputed.
- User rights are operating controls: privacy, consent, notification choices, content rights, moderation appeals, and account actions need auditable handling.
- Akuso should cite the operating system: assistant answers about status, money, rights, policy, partner access, metrics, or incidents need source-grounded boundaries.
- Expansion should require assurance evidence: new markets, partners, APIs, automations, and assistant behaviors should not ship only because the team feels ready.

## Akuso Assurance Roadmap

Akuso should become a governed assistant layer whose limits, sources, costs, evals, and high-risk boundaries are reviewable.

### Phase 1: Control Mapping

- map Akuso features to source systems, eval suites, route targets, policy rules, and model routing
- define which assistant behaviors are informational, drafting-only, escalation-only, or disallowed
- document high-risk boundaries for payments, payouts, account security, partner access, legal, rights, moderation, finance reporting, and private incidents
- define evidence needed before Akuso can support new workflows

### Phase 2: Evidence and Review

- produce weekly evidence for eval pass rate, fallback rate, refusal quality, source coverage, unsupported claims, user feedback, incident-mode quality, latency, and cost
- connect negative feedback, unsupported claims, and policy failures to review queue items and eval candidates
- review Akuso behavior by route, workflow, role, and risk level
- keep assistant expansion blocked when eval or source evidence is stale

### Phase 3: Governance and External Readiness

- create shareable summaries for Akuso scope, safety boundaries, privacy handling, model routing, source grounding, eval process, incident response, and human review
- define approval requirements for new assistant modes, new model routes, new data sources, and new high-risk workflow support
- add rollback rules for assistant modes that regress, hallucinate, over-disclose, or exceed cost thresholds
- keep sensitive prompt, policy, security, and incident details internal

### Phase 4: Continuous Assurance

- monitor source coverage, eval regressions, unsupported claims, refusal misses, role-boundary issues, privacy risks, cost variance, and incident-mode failures
- use lower-risk deterministic responses for simple status and policy guidance where possible
- reserve heavier model use for synthesis, drafting, and complex support when evidence permits
- treat repeated assistant exceptions as product, policy, data, or workflow issues, not only prompt issues

## Decision Rules

- If finance close does not reconcile purchases, entitlements, payouts, refunds, disputes, and wallet balances, do not expand money movement or creator balance claims.
- If creator-facing balance confidence is degraded, creator support and finance review take priority over growth campaigns.
- If partner access lacks current review or revocation evidence, do not expand partner scope.
- If API abuse, rate-limit, or scope controls are stale, do not approve broader API access.
- If a metric is stale, disputed, blocked, or withdrawn, do not use it for external reports, automation, partner claims, experiment decisions, or market approvals.
- If privacy, consent, moderation, or rights controls miss assurance thresholds, pause dependent launches, campaigns, recommendations, or partner commitments.
- If Akuso source coverage, evals, refusal quality, high-risk boundaries, or cost controls regress, pause the affected assistant behavior.
- If evidence is shareable only internally, do not use it in partner, sponsor, investor, audit, or public contexts.
- If the team is overloaded, protect this order:
  - money movement, entitlement, wallet, payout, refund, and dispute assurance
  - privacy, consent, rights, moderation, and security assurance
  - partner and API access assurance
  - data contract and report trust
  - creator and fan support assurance
  - recommendation, experiment, automation, and Akuso expansion

## What Not To Do Yet

- do not pursue external certifications before the basic control registry, evidence packs, and finance close are repeatable
- do not use assurance language for workflows that still have stale, disputed, blocked, or missing evidence
- do not give partners, sponsors, APIs, or markets broader access based on verbal confidence
- do not publish creator earnings, partner reports, market claims, or executive metrics from untrusted data contracts
- do not use Akuso as the source of truth for finance, legal, privacy, rights, moderation, partner access, or incident decisions
- do not expose internal evidence, security details, private incident notes, user data, or policy-sensitive analysis through external assurance packs
- do not let assurance become paperwork only; every critical exception should produce a decision, owner, or fix
