# Tengacion Network 30/60/90 Day Execution Roadmap

Follow-on roadmap:
[Tengacion Intelligence 30/60/90 Day Execution Roadmap](./tengacion-intelligence-30-60-90-roadmap.md)

## Purpose

This roadmap is the seventh execution cycle after the foundation, operating-system, scale, expansion, platform, and ecosystem roadmaps. The earlier cycles made Tengacion usable, operable, launchable, expandable, platformized, and ecosystem-ready. This cycle is about turning that ecosystem into a trusted network:

1. Creator business networks
2. Fan community and advocacy loops
3. Partner and channel integrations
4. Network-scale finance and settlement
5. Data products, AI intelligence, governance, and resilience

The goal is to make Tengacion useful beyond first-party workflows. Creators should be able to find business support, collaborators, partners, fans, and repeatable growth paths. Fans should be able to deepen relationships with creators and communities without being pushed into spammy loops. Partners should be able to operate through scoped, revocable, privacy-safe integrations. The team should be able to grow the network without losing control of money movement, consent, rights, safety, or Akuso quality.

## Starting baseline

This plan assumes the previous roadmap has produced these anchors:

- creator service programs with eligibility, completion, and outcome tracking
- selected fan community loops with notification, referral, and abuse guardrails
- partner integration levels with access controls, consent, exports, revocation, and audit logs
- ecosystem health and finance reporting across creators, fans, partners, sponsors, campaigns, markets, and support load
- multi-market readiness gates for payment, payout, creator supply, fan demand, support, moderation, rights, and privacy
- Akuso ecosystem coverage for creator services, partner workflows, finance explanations, market readiness, and governance summaries
- response playbooks for partner access revocation, sponsored campaign complaints, creator service misinformation, referral abuse, cross-market payout blockers, and rights takedown surges

The remaining problem is network leverage. Tengacion should help trustworthy participants create value together while keeping the platform explainable, auditable, and resilient.

## North-star outcomes for the next 90 days

By the end of this plan, Tengacion should have:

- a creator business network model for services, collaborators, events, education, and monetization support
- fan community programs that improve advocacy, repeat participation, and creator discovery without harming trust
- partner and channel integration workflows that can move from manual reports to scoped exports or APIs only when ready
- network finance reporting that reconciles creator earnings, partner revenue, sponsor revenue, service fees, payouts, refunds, disputes, and cost proxies
- privacy-safe data products for creator benchmarks, partner reports, cohort insights, and internal network decisions
- multi-market operating gates that can compare communities and channels without flattening local constraints
- Akuso grounded in network objects, permissions, service scopes, partner access levels, and escalation rules
- a clear decision on which network loops are ready to scale, which should stay controlled, and which should be retired

## Product principles

- Network growth must improve creator outcomes: every new partner, service, integration, or fan loop should have a creator benefit that can be measured.
- Consent is a product primitive: creator participation, partner access, sponsor reporting, fan visibility, and service provider involvement require explicit boundaries.
- Aggregation is the default: creators, partners, and sponsors get useful patterns, not private fan-level behavior.
- Access should graduate slowly: manual report before export, export before dashboard access, dashboard before API candidate.
- Money movement stays ledger-first: network revenue, service fees, payouts, reversals, refunds, and partner settlements must reconcile before they drive decisions.
- Community loops need brakes: referrals, clubs, milestones, reminders, and creator messages need suppression rules, complaint monitoring, and abuse review.
- Akuso explains and drafts, but does not approve finance, legal, moderation, account, partner access, payout, or market launch decisions.

## First 30 days

### Theme

Define the network model before scaling access.

### Product goals

- define creator business network programs and access rules
- design fan community loops with visible trust controls
- specify partner and channel integration graduation paths
- build the network finance and settlement model
- define privacy-safe data products and Akuso network grounding

### Deliverables

#### 1. Define the creator business network model

- Create network program types:
  - launch coaching
  - catalog quality support
  - rights and takedown support
  - live event programming
  - creator collaboration
  - marketplace or campaign readiness
  - subscription growth review
  - pricing and packaging review
  - external service provider referral
- Each program should define:
  - eligibility
  - creator consent
  - support owner
  - provider or partner involvement
  - required data
  - service scope
  - expected outcome
  - fee or cost treatment
  - success metric
  - graduation condition
  - escalation path
- Separate internal creator support from paid, partner-led, or provider-led services.

### Exit criteria

- creator business network programs are named and scoped
- service ownership, consent, and cost treatment are explicit
- creators are not pushed into partner or provider workflows without clear boundaries

#### 2. Design fan community and advocacy loops

- Define community loops for:
  - supporter club updates
  - creator milestone celebrations
  - fan referral after high-satisfaction actions
  - live event follow-up
  - saved content completion
  - subscription renewal recovery
  - fan-to-fan campaign sharing
  - similar creator discovery after purchase
- Add guardrails for:
  - notification frequency
  - repeated ignored prompts
  - referral abuse
  - creator message volume
  - report or complaint spikes
  - sensitive content categories
  - refund or dispute context
  - dormant or at-risk fans
- Define what can be visible to creators:
  - aggregate community movement
  - opt-in fan signals
  - campaign-level performance
  - privacy-safe referral totals

### Exit criteria

- fan community loops are designed with suppression and abuse controls
- creator dashboards avoid exposing private fan behavior
- referral and advocacy work has trust guardrails before scale

#### 3. Specify partner and channel integration graduation

- Define integration levels:
  - manual report
  - scheduled export
  - scoped dashboard access
  - campaign collaboration
  - sponsor package
  - API candidate
  - approved API integration
- Each level should define:
  - allowed data
  - prohibited data
  - approval owner
  - creator consent requirement
  - sponsor labeling requirement
  - revocation path
  - audit event
  - data retention rule
  - renewal review
- Add hard blockers for:
  - private fan-level behavior
  - payment identifiers
  - identity verification data
  - moderation-sensitive detail
  - unsupported partner claims
  - unreconciled finance metrics

### Exit criteria

- partner access has a clear graduation path
- API candidates cannot skip export, consent, audit, and privacy review
- partner-facing data remains aggregated and revocable by default

#### 4. Build the network finance and settlement blueprint

- Define reporting dimensions:
  - creator
  - creator service program
  - external provider
  - fan cohort
  - partner
  - sponsor package
  - campaign
  - offer
  - market or community
  - acquisition channel
- Include:
  - GMV
  - creator earnings
  - platform commission
  - service fees
  - partner revenue
  - sponsor revenue
  - payment fees
  - refunds
  - disputes
  - payout status
  - provider settlement status
  - support cost proxy
  - infrastructure and model cost proxy
  - contribution margin
- Keep ledger reconciliation as the source of truth for settlement decisions.

### Exit criteria

- network finance has a clear dimensional model
- partner, sponsor, provider, and creator economics can be separated
- settlement reports cannot outrun ledger reconciliation

#### 5. Define network data products and Akuso grounding

- Define data products for:
  - creator benchmark summaries
  - creator opportunity scoring
  - fan community health
  - partner-safe campaign summaries
  - market readiness comparisons
  - contribution margin views
  - support and moderation load forecasts
  - Akuso network quality summaries
- For each product, define:
  - owner
  - audience
  - data sources
  - privacy limits
  - data quality checks
  - refresh cadence
  - decision it supports
  - export rules
- Ground Akuso in:
  - network program scopes
  - partner integration levels
  - consent states
  - finance report limitations
  - escalation paths
  - unsupported action refusals

### Exit criteria

- data products are mapped to real decisions
- privacy and data quality checks are designed before external sharing
- Akuso can explain network workflows without inventing access, benefits, or authority

### Day 30 exit criteria

- creator business network model is defined
- fan community and advocacy loops are designed with brakes
- partner and channel integration graduation is documented
- network finance and settlement blueprint is ready
- data products and Akuso grounding scope are specified

## Akuso network roadmap

Akuso should help users understand the network without becoming the network operator.

### Phase 1: Network grounding

- ground answers in creator programs, fan community loops, partner access levels, consent states, finance report limits, and readiness gates
- explain what each workflow can and cannot do
- route finance, legal, payout, moderation, account, partner access, API access, and market launch actions to secure review flows
- cite the relevant creator, admin, partner, support, or governance surface when possible

### Phase 2: Creator, fan, and partner assistance

- help creators understand program eligibility, service scope, collaboration options, pricing reviews, and campaign readiness
- help fans understand community features, subscriptions, reminders, referrals, and support paths
- help admins summarize partner state, access level, consent requirements, report readiness, and renewal signals
- keep external, partner-facing, public, financial, legal, and moderation-impacting text review-gated

### Phase 3: Network operations intelligence

- summarize creator program outcomes, fan community health, partner access risk, finance movement, support load, and market readiness
- flag missing data before making recommendations
- escalate conflicting metrics or risky patterns to human review
- preserve source context for audit and follow-up

### Phase 4: Eval, privacy, and cost discipline

- add evals for creator service claims, fan privacy, partner access, finance escalation, API boundaries, market readiness, and export refusal
- monitor helpfulness by creator, fan, support, admin, partner, and governance workflow
- keep lightweight paths for simple explanations
- reserve heavier model use for synthesis, conflicting-signal review, and high-context admin summaries

## Decision rules

- If creator programs do not improve creator outcomes, simplify or pause them.
- If fan community loops increase opt-outs, complaints, abuse, or support load, reduce scope before expanding.
- If partner access cannot be scoped, audited, and revoked, do not expand access.
- If finance or settlement reporting does not reconcile to ledgers, do not use it for partner, provider, or executive decisions.
- If sponsor or partner revenue increases recommendation distrust, pause sponsored or partner boosts.
- If payout, refund, dispute, or rights pressure rises, slow monetization and partner expansion.
- If market readiness gates fail, keep the market in research, seed, or hold.
- If API candidate gates are incomplete, keep the workflow manual, export-based, or dashboard-only.
- If Akuso network evals regress, pause the affected workflow.
- If support or moderation SLAs fall behind, do not add new partner, community, or market launches.
- If the team is overloaded, protect this order:
  - money movement and content access
  - privacy, consent, and partner access controls
  - support, moderation, and rights response
  - creator trust and earnings
  - fan community health
  - partner, sponsor, and channel expansion
  - data products, APIs, automation, and Akuso expansion

## What not to do yet

- do not turn informal creator support into paid services without clear scope, consent, and outcomes
- do not expose fan-level private behavior to creators, partners, sponsors, providers, or external channels
- do not build broad public APIs before manual, export, and dashboard workflows prove the access model
- do not expand partner access without consent, audit, privacy review, revocation, and renewal checks
- do not use network finance reports for decisions before ledger reconciliation is confirmed
- do not scale fan community loops that create spam, abuse, support load, or trust complaints
- do not let Akuso make finance, legal, moderation, account, payout, partner access, API access, or market launch decisions
