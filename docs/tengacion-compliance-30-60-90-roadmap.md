## Purpose

This roadmap is the thirteenth execution cycle after the foundation, operating-system, scale, expansion, platform, ecosystem, network, intelligence, automation, orchestration, resilience, and assurance roadmaps. The earlier cycles made Tengacion usable, operable, launchable, expandable, platformized, ecosystem-ready, network-aware, intelligence-led, automation-ready, orchestration-capable, resilience-tested, and assurance-backed. This cycle is about turning assurance into a compliance operating system:

1. Obligation ownership and policy governance
2. Privacy, data protection, security, and vendor compliance
3. Finance, payout, creator earnings, tax, and consumer protection compliance
4. Content rights, moderation, market, partner, and API compliance
5. Akuso, AI governance, model risk, and audit operations

The goal is not to declare Tengacion compliant by writing documents. The goal is to make every important obligation traceable to an owner, policy, control, evidence source, review cadence, exception path, and readiness decision. Legal and regulatory interpretation should still be reviewed by qualified counsel for each market, partner, and product commitment.

## Starting Baseline

This plan assumes the previous roadmap has produced these anchors:

- an assurance control registry for money movement, entitlement, payout, refund, dispute, partner access, API access, data products, privacy, rights, moderation, recommendations, notifications, experiments, and Akuso
- evidence packs with owners, source systems, freshness expectations, exception status, reviewer notes, approval history, and sharing levels
- monthly finance and revenue assurance close covering purchases, entitlements, payouts, refunds, disputes, commissions, fees, and creator-facing balances
- partner, sponsor, API, and market readiness packets for due diligence
- data contracts and metric trust states for executive, creator, partner, finance, recommendation, experimentation, and automation metrics
- auditable trust controls for privacy, consent, rights, moderation, recommendation complaints, notifications, referrals, and support escalation
- Akuso governance evidence for evals, source coverage, model routing, costs, refusals, memory scope, incident mode, and high-risk workflow boundaries

The remaining problem is formal compliance maturity. Tengacion may have evidence, but the team still needs a living obligation register, policy library, review calendar, vendor and data inventory, training program, audit workflow, and legal escalation path that can survive new markets, partners, payment flows, creator categories, data products, APIs, automations, and AI features.

## North-star Outcomes For The Next 90 Days

By the end of this plan, Tengacion should have:

- a compliance obligation register mapped by market, user type, workflow, data category, partner type, payment flow, content type, and Akuso capability
- a policy library covering privacy, data retention, security access, payments, payouts, refunds, disputes, creator earnings, taxes or fees, content rights, moderation, partner data sharing, APIs, experiments, recommendations, automation, and AI governance
- a data inventory showing personal data, creator business data, payment metadata, support data, content metadata, recommendation data, partner-exported data, Akuso context, retention rules, deletion rules, and access scopes
- vendor and subprocessor governance for payment providers, storage providers, analytics tools, model providers, notification providers, support tools, and partner integrations
- compliance-ready finance operations for creator earnings, payout eligibility, refund and dispute handling, settlement support, platform fees, and creator-facing statements
- market, partner, sponsor, and API approval workflows that require policy, privacy, data, security, finance, content, and AI review where relevant
- content rights and moderation compliance workflows for takedowns, appeals, repeat infringement patterns, creator disputes, adult or restricted content, and recommendation complaints
- Akuso AI governance for capability approval, source grounding, evaluation, human review, incident response, privacy boundaries, model routing, prompt changes, and audit evidence
- a compliance review board and audit calendar that convert obligations, evidence gaps, incidents, and exceptions into owned remediation

## Product Principles

- Compliance is an operating habit: obligations should be reviewed, assigned, evidenced, trained, and updated, not filed away.
- Policies need controls: every meaningful policy should map to a product control, operational step, data rule, or review gate.
- Market expansion requires local review: do not assume a control that works in one market is enough for another market.
- Data minimization should shape the product: collect, expose, export, and retain only what the workflow needs.
- Money claims need reconciliation: creator earnings, platform fees, payouts, refunds, disputes, and statements should be backed by finance evidence.
- Partner access must stay scoped: every partner, sponsor, export, dashboard, and API path needs purpose, permission, expiration, and revocation.
- Content governance must be explainable: rights, moderation, recommendation, appeal, and enforcement decisions need evidence and review paths.
- AI governance is product governance: Akuso changes should be reviewed like workflow changes when they affect user trust, money, rights, support, or safety.

## Akuso Compliance Roadmap

Akuso should be governed as a product capability, not treated as a separate experiment outside compliance operations.

### Phase 1: AI Obligation And Capability Mapping

- inventory every Akuso mode, route, source system, model route, user role, memory behavior, and high-risk boundary
- map capabilities to policies, controls, eval suites, logging requirements, human review needs, and privacy constraints
- define which capabilities are user-facing, admin-facing, creator-facing, partner-facing, internal-only, or blocked
- require compliance review before Akuso expands into money movement, rights, moderation, partner, market, legal, finance, or private incident workflows

### Phase 2: Source, Privacy, And Eval Evidence

- track source coverage, freshness, and route accuracy
- verify memory redaction, role scoping, retention, deletion, and privacy request handling
- run evals for unsupported finance claims, private data exposure, policy overreach, unsafe refusal misses, rights and moderation boundaries, partner data boundaries, and incident-mode guidance
- connect Akuso feedback, unsupported claims, and safety issues to remediation and eval fixtures

### Phase 3: Model, Vendor, And Change Governance

- review model providers, data sharing, retention, routing, costs, latency, and fallback behavior
- require approval for new models, new prompts, new source systems, new tools, new memory scopes, and new high-risk workflows
- keep prompt and policy change history reviewable
- define rollback for regressions, cost breaches, unsupported claims, or privacy issues

### Phase 4: Audit And External Communication

- produce external-safe summaries for Akuso scope, privacy boundaries, source grounding, eval process, refusal boundaries, human review, incident response, and cost controls
- keep sensitive prompts, security details, private incidents, user data, and policy-sensitive analysis internal
- avoid representing Akuso as a decision-maker for finance, legal, rights, moderation, partner access, or account security
- review all partner-facing, market-facing, or investor-facing AI claims against current evidence

## Decision Rules

- If the obligation is unclear, get qualified review before treating the workflow as ready.
- If a policy has no control, do not claim the policy is operational.
- If a control has no evidence, do not use it to approve expansion.
- If data purpose, retention, access, or sharing is unclear, do not expand collection, export, API access, automation, or Akuso grounding.
- If finance, payout, refund, dispute, subscription, pricing, or creator statement obligations are unresolved, pause affected money movement expansion.
- If rights, moderation, appeal, or restricted-content controls are missing, pause affected content category expansion.
- If partner or API revocation is not tested, do not expand external access.
- If a market launch has unresolved privacy, finance, content, support, partner, or Akuso blockers, do not launch that market.
- If Akuso governance evidence is stale, do not expand the affected assistant behavior.
- If the team is overloaded, protect this order:
  - privacy, security, data protection, and access controls
  - payment, payout, refund, dispute, creator earnings, and subscription controls
  - rights, moderation, content, and user support controls
  - partner, API, export, and external reporting controls
  - market readiness and local review
  - recommendations, experiments, automation, and Akuso expansion

## What Not To Do Yet

- do not claim legal compliance from internal planning alone
- do not expand into a market without local review of obligations, policies, controls, and evidence
- do not publish user, creator, partner, finance, market, or AI claims from stale or disputed evidence
- do not collect, export, retain, or ground Akuso on data without a clear purpose and access scope
- do not let partner or API access continue without current review and revocation ability
- do not use Akuso to make final decisions about finance, legal, rights, moderation, partner access, account security, privacy, or market readiness
- do not bury accepted risk without an owner, expiration, compensating control, and re-review trigger
- do not turn compliance into paperwork without product, operational, or policy changes
