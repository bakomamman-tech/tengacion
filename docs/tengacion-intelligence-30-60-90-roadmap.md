# Tengacion Intelligence 30/60/90 Day Execution Roadmap

Follow-on roadmap:
[Tengacion Automation 30/60/90 Day Execution Roadmap](./tengacion-automation-30-60-90-roadmap.md)

## Purpose

This roadmap is the eighth execution cycle after the foundation, operating-system, scale, expansion, platform, ecosystem, and network roadmaps. The earlier cycles made Tengacion usable, operable, launchable, expandable, platformized, ecosystem-ready, and network-aware. This cycle is about turning that network into an intelligence layer:

1. Trusted data products
2. Creator, fan, partner, and finance intelligence
3. Governed APIs and integration readiness
4. Predictive operations and risk monitoring
5. Akuso as a measured intelligence copilot

The goal is not to automate every decision. The goal is to make the right decisions easier, faster, safer, and more repeatable. Tengacion should be able to explain what is working, what is risky, what is ready to scale, what should pause, and where creators, fans, partners, and internal teams need help next.

## Starting baseline

This plan assumes the previous roadmap has produced these anchors:

- creator business network programs with consent, service scope, outcomes, and graduation decisions
- controlled fan community and advocacy loops with suppression, complaint, and abuse controls
- partner and channel integration levels with scoped access, consent, exports, dashboard access, API candidate gates, and revocation paths
- network finance and settlement reporting that separates creator, provider, partner, sponsor, campaign, market, and platform economics
- network intelligence dashboard or specification across creator programs, fan community, partners, finance, trust, support, moderation, recommendation health, and Akuso quality
- data products for creator benchmarks, opportunity scoring, market readiness, partner-safe summaries, and margin views
- Akuso network grounding, evals, escalation rules, privacy boundaries, and cost-awareness

The remaining problem is decision quality. Tengacion now has many loops, actors, and reports. The next step is making the intelligence behind those loops trusted enough for weekly decisions, partner expansion, creator support, risk review, and future automation.

## North-star outcomes for the next 90 days

By the end of this plan, Tengacion should have:

- documented metric contracts and data lineage for the most important network decisions
- creator intelligence that helps creators choose the next best earning, catalog, collaboration, or audience action
- fan intelligence that improves lifecycle, community, and recommendation decisions while preserving privacy
- partner and API intelligence that separates ready integrations from risky or premature ones
- finance and margin intelligence that reconciles before shaping strategy
- predictive operations signals for support, moderation, payout, entitlement, partner access, and rights pressure
- Akuso able to summarize, explain, and draft from trusted intelligence without acting as the decision authority
- a clear readiness decision for which intelligence products can become self-serve, external, API-backed, or automation-assisted

## Product principles

- Intelligence must cite its source: every important recommendation needs a metric, timeframe, owner, and confidence signal.
- Data quality beats dashboard volume: unreliable metrics should be flagged, not beautified.
- Predictions suggest, humans decide: forecasts and risk scores guide review; they do not approve payouts, refunds, moderation actions, API access, or partner launches.
- Privacy stays structural: fan-level private behavior remains protected even when intelligence becomes more useful.
- Creator benefit is the first test: creator intelligence should help creators earn, retain fans, reduce confusion, or improve catalog quality.
- Finance intelligence remains ledger-first: margin, settlement, partner, and sponsor decisions must reconcile before they are externalized.
- Akuso can explain intelligence, but cannot invent evidence, hide uncertainty, or bypass governance.

## First 30 days

### Theme

Make intelligence trustworthy before making it powerful.

### Product goals

- define metric contracts and data trust states
- turn existing network reports into governed data products
- specify creator, fan, partner, finance, and operations intelligence outputs
- define Akuso source-grounded intelligence behavior
- decide what is internal-only, creator-facing, partner-facing, or API-eligible

### Deliverables

#### 1. Establish metric contracts and trust states

- Define contracts for:
  - GMV
  - creator earnings
  - platform commission
  - service revenue
  - partner revenue
  - sponsor revenue
  - contribution margin
  - payout status
  - refund and dispute rate
  - creator program outcomes
  - fan lifecycle movement
  - community participation
  - recommendation trust
  - support and moderation load
  - Akuso quality
- Each contract should include:
  - owner
  - source tables or events
  - calculation rules
  - freshness expectation
  - known limitations
  - privacy classification
  - decision it supports
  - export eligibility
- Add trust states:
  - trusted
  - watch
  - stale
  - disputed
  - blocked

### Exit criteria

- core network metrics have owners and definitions
- dashboards can show when a metric should not be trusted
- disputed metrics do not silently drive decisions

#### 2. Define governed intelligence products

- Create product definitions for:
  - creator opportunity score
  - creator benchmark summary
  - catalog health intelligence
  - fan community health score
  - lifecycle movement summary
  - partner readiness score
  - API candidate readiness score
  - margin confidence summary
  - support and moderation risk forecast
  - Akuso intelligence quality summary
- Each product should define:
  - audience
  - owner
  - refresh cadence
  - source metrics
  - confidence signal
  - privacy limit
  - recommended action
  - review owner
  - rollback or withdrawal path

### Exit criteria

- intelligence products are tied to decisions, not just dashboards
- external or creator-facing products have privacy and confidence limits
- weak data products can be withdrawn without breaking core workflows

#### 3. Design creator intelligence surfaces

- Define creator-facing intelligence for:
  - what catalog item to improve next
  - which offer or launch type fits current readiness
  - which subscription benefit needs clearer packaging
  - which community loop is helping retention
  - whether collaboration or cross-promotion is a good fit
  - whether pricing, preview, metadata, or cover art needs review
- Define admin-facing intelligence for:
  - creator stage
  - creator opportunity
  - creator support risk
  - payout or finance blocker
  - service program fit
  - high-potential creator watchlist
- Keep recommendations explainable and reversible.

### Exit criteria

- creator intelligence helps choose a practical next action
- admin and creator views use the same underlying definitions
- Akuso can explain the recommendation without overstating certainty

#### 4. Design partner, API, and export intelligence

- Define readiness signals for:
  - scheduled export
  - scoped dashboard access
  - partner campaign collaboration
  - sponsor package renewal
  - API candidate review
  - approved API integration
- Include:
  - consent completeness
  - data quality
  - export reliability
  - audit completeness
  - security review status
  - privacy review status
  - finance reconciliation status
  - complaint or support load
  - revocation test status
  - renewal signal
- Keep API readiness separate from partner enthusiasm.

### Exit criteria

- partner access upgrades are evidence-based
- API candidates cannot move forward with missing consent, audit, privacy, or revocation evidence
- partner reports remain privacy-safe and finance-aware

#### 5. Define Akuso intelligence behavior

- Akuso should:
  - summarize trusted metrics with timeframe and source context
  - explain confidence and limitations
  - suggest reviewable next actions
  - route sensitive decisions to secure workflows
  - flag stale, disputed, or blocked metrics
  - refuse unsupported data access or private fan-level requests
- Akuso should not:
  - invent missing metrics
  - hide low confidence
  - approve payouts, refunds, takedowns, exports, API access, or partner launches
  - expose private user behavior
  - make legal, finance, moderation, or compliance determinations

### Exit criteria

- Akuso intelligence behavior has clear source, confidence, and refusal rules
- eval coverage is scoped before intelligence summaries expand
- sensitive decisions remain human-owned and app-authorized

### Day 30 exit criteria

- metric contracts and trust states are documented
- governed intelligence products are defined
- creator, partner, API, finance, and operations intelligence surfaces are specified
- Akuso source-grounded intelligence behavior is defined
- internal, creator-facing, partner-facing, and API-eligible boundaries are clear

## Akuso intelligence roadmap

Akuso should make trusted intelligence understandable without becoming the authority behind the intelligence.

### Phase 1: Source-grounded summaries

- summarize only supported metrics and known data products
- show timeframe, source context, confidence, and limitations
- flag stale, disputed, or blocked metrics
- refuse private fan-level, finance-sensitive, partner-restricted, or unsupported data requests

### Phase 2: Decision support

- explain creator opportunity, partner readiness, finance confidence, fan community health, and operational risk
- suggest next actions that stay inside app permissions and governance rules
- draft review notes, support replies, and partner-safe summaries from approved data
- keep final decisions human-owned

### Phase 3: Intelligence operations

- summarize data quality issues, metric disputes, stale dashboards, export failures, API risks, and eval regressions
- identify missing data before making recommendations
- escalate conflicting signals to human review
- preserve source links and audit context

### Phase 4: Eval, privacy, and cost discipline

- add evals for citation quality, stale metric handling, fan privacy, finance caveats, partner API boundaries, and unsupported automation refusal
- monitor helpfulness, correction rate, fallback rate, source-context coverage, and cost by workflow
- route simple explanations to lighter paths
- reserve heavier model use for synthesis, conflicting evidence, and high-context reviews

## Decision rules

- If a metric is stale, disputed, or blocked, do not use it for external reports, partner decisions, API access, or automation.
- If creator intelligence does not produce useful action, simplify the recommendation before expanding it.
- If fan intelligence increases opt-outs, complaints, or abuse, reduce scope and review suppression rules.
- If finance intelligence does not reconcile, keep it out of partner, sponsor, provider, and executive decisions.
- If API readiness lacks consent, revocation, audit, privacy, security, or rate-limit evidence, keep the workflow manual or export-only.
- If predictive warnings create too much noise, tune or narrow them before adding more warnings.
- If Akuso intelligence evals regress, pause the affected summaries or recommendations.
- If partner-facing intelligence risks private fan behavior, aggregate further or do not ship it.
- If the team is overloaded, protect this order:
  - metric correctness and data quality
  - privacy, consent, and finance reconciliation
  - creator-facing intelligence
  - partner and API readiness
  - predictive operations
  - Akuso expansion and cost optimization
