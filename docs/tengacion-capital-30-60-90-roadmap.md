# Tengacion Capital 30/60/90 Day Execution Roadmap

## Purpose

This roadmap is the seventeenth execution cycle after the foundation, operating-system, scale, expansion, platform, ecosystem, network, intelligence, automation, orchestration, resilience, assurance, compliance, audit, certification, and institutional roadmaps. The earlier cycles made Tengacion usable, operable, launchable, expandable, platformized, ecosystem-ready, network-aware, intelligence-led, automation-ready, orchestration-capable, resilience-tested, assurance-backed, compliance-mapped, audit-ready, certification-oriented, and institutionally governable. This cycle is about turning institutional readiness into disciplined capital strategy:

1. Capital strategy, runway, and financing readiness
2. Investor, strategic partner, and institutional diligence operations
3. Financial controls, unit economics, creator economics, and capital allocation
4. Growth investment, market expansion, enterprise commitments, and risk governance
5. Akuso cost discipline, AI investment governance, and AI claim accountability

The goal is not to raise money for its own sake or to make unsupported financial claims. The goal is to understand what Tengacion can responsibly fund, what it should prove before seeking capital, which investor or strategic paths match the business, and how each capital commitment affects creators, fans, partners, controls, product quality, and Akuso governance. Fundraising, securities, tax, and legal decisions should be reviewed by qualified advisors.

## Starting Baseline

This plan assumes the previous roadmap has produced these anchors:

- an institutional governance calendar for executive, finance, risk, trust, security, privacy, partner, market, creator, and Akuso AI reviews
- decision records for major product, market, partner, API, finance, security, privacy, content, recommendation, automation, and Akuso commitments
- an investor and institutional data room with approved financial, product, traction, risk, legal, compliance, security, privacy, partner, creator, and AI governance materials
- finance operating discipline for unit economics, creator earnings confidence, payout exposure, refund and dispute reserves, revenue quality, cost controls, and capital planning
- risk appetite statements for money movement, data sharing, partner access, market launch, content categories, recommendations, automation, and Akuso behavior
- management reporting that separates trusted metrics, disputed metrics, leading indicators, lagging indicators, commitments, risks, and unresolved exceptions
- Akuso institutional governance for AI risk appetite, capability approvals, model/vendor oversight, prompt and policy controls, eval board reporting, incident escalation, and external claim review

The remaining problem is capital discipline. Tengacion may be ready for serious institutional conversations, but the team still needs a clear financing thesis, defensible financial model, investor segmentation, strategic partner fit, capital allocation rules, milestone-based spend plan, risk-adjusted growth plan, and governance process that prevents money from amplifying weak controls.

## North-star Outcomes For The Next 90 Days

By the end of this plan, Tengacion should have:

- a capital strategy that explains whether Tengacion should pursue bootstrap discipline, revenue financing, strategic partner funding, grants, venture funding, enterprise prepayments, creator institution partnerships, or delayed fundraising
- a financing readiness scorecard covering traction, revenue quality, creator economics, unit economics, retention, payout confidence, trust controls, market readiness, partner pipeline, security/privacy posture, and Akuso governance
- a financial model with conservative, base, and upside scenarios for GMV, revenue, creator earnings, payouts, refunds, disputes, infrastructure cost, support cost, moderation cost, Akuso cost, partner cost, hiring, runway, and milestone burn
- a capital allocation framework that ties spend to verified learning, creator outcomes, revenue quality, risk reduction, market readiness, and operating leverage
- an investor and strategic partner pipeline segmented by fit, check size, strategic value, diligence burden, control expectations, creator alignment, AI appetite, and market leverage
- diligence-ready packets for investor, strategic partner, enterprise, payment provider, creator institution, market stakeholder, and AI governance conversations
- a capital risk register that tracks dilution risk, control risk, revenue quality risk, creator trust risk, payout exposure, refund/dispute exposure, market risk, partner concentration, data/security risk, and Akuso cost or safety risk
- governance for investor claims, financial metrics, projections, data-room sharing, diligence Q&A, and external AI claims
- a decision on whether to fundraise, pursue strategic capital, pursue revenue-backed growth, delay capital and prove more, or run a targeted partner financing path

## Product Principles

- Capital should amplify proof, not hide uncertainty: spend should follow validated creator, fan, revenue, and trust signals.
- Runway is a product constraint: hiring, infrastructure, AI, support, moderation, and market spend should match the operating plan.
- Creator economics are core economics: creator earnings confidence, payout health, take rate, refunds, disputes, and retention matter as much as top-line GMV.
- Capital claims must be evidence-backed: investor and partner materials should use approved metrics with confidence states and clear definitions.
- Strategic money has strings: partner capital, enterprise prepayments, sponsorships, grants, or platform deals should be reviewed for control, exclusivity, data, content, and AI implications.
- Spend should have gates: each major spend category needs milestone triggers, stop-loss rules, owners, and review dates.
- AI spend needs governance: Akuso model cost, eval quality, source coverage, safety, and user value should guide AI investment decisions.
- Do not confuse fundraising with progress: if metrics, controls, or trust are weak, the better move may be to harden the business before opening a round.

## Akuso Capital Governance Roadmap

Akuso should be treated as both a product advantage and a cost/risk center that needs disciplined capital allocation.

### Phase 1: AI Cost And Value Baseline

- measure Akuso cost by route, mode, model, workflow, user role, and output type
- compare cost against:
  - creator workflow value
  - support deflection
  - onboarding completion
  - purchase guidance quality
  - payout readiness guidance quality
  - admin review efficiency
  - incident support value
- separate experimental AI spend from production AI spend
- define budget thresholds and stop-loss rules

### Phase 2: AI Investment Gates

- require evidence before expanding:
  - new models
  - new source systems
  - new memory scopes
  - new high-risk workflow support
  - new partner-facing summaries
  - new market-facing AI claims
- gate expansion on:
  - eval pass rate
  - source coverage
  - unsupported claim rate
  - refusal quality
  - privacy and memory safety
  - latency
  - cost per useful workflow
  - incident history

### Phase 3: AI Diligence Story

- prepare investor and strategic partner answers for:
  - what Akuso does
  - what Akuso does not do
  - why Akuso improves Tengacion
  - how Akuso is evaluated
  - how Akuso costs are controlled
  - how privacy and memory are handled
  - how high-risk workflows are blocked
  - how incidents and feedback improve the system
- keep claims narrow and tied to current evidence

### Phase 4: AI Capital Allocation

- allocate AI spend by:
  - creator outcome impact
  - support outcome impact
  - revenue or retention signal
  - safety and governance need
  - infrastructure leverage
  - cost efficiency
- pause AI investment where:
  - evals are stale
  - source coverage is weak
  - unsupported claims rise
  - cost exceeds value
  - privacy or refusal boundaries fail
  - external claims outrun evidence

## Decision Rules

- If financial metrics are disputed or not externally approved, do not use them in investor or partner materials.
- If creator earnings confidence is weak, do not make creator economics claims.
- If payout, refund, or dispute exposure is unclear, do not claim money movement readiness.
- If unit economics depend on unsupported assumptions, label them clearly and keep them out of approved external claims.
- If data-room evidence is stale, pause sharing until refreshed.
- If strategic partner money requires unsafe data sharing, exclusivity, control, or AI claims, reject or renegotiate.
- If fundraising would distract from urgent product, trust, finance, or resilience gaps, delay capital outreach.
- If Akuso cost exceeds value or governance evidence is stale, pause AI expansion and narrow external AI claims.
- If the team is overloaded, protect this order:
  - runway, revenue quality, creator earnings, payout exposure, refund/dispute reserves
  - data-room accuracy and external claims
  - product proof and creator/fan retention
  - security, privacy, compliance, and trust blockers
  - strategic partner diligence and terms
  - Akuso AI investment and claims

## What Not To Do Yet

- do not start broad fundraising before the capital thesis, financial model, data room, and claims governance are ready
- do not pitch disputed or estimated metrics as facts
- do not hide creator earnings, payout, refund, dispute, support, moderation, infrastructure, or Akuso costs from the model
- do not treat strategic partner money as free if it creates data, control, exclusivity, rights, market, or AI risk
- do not use capital to scale workflows that are failing trust, finance, privacy, security, or Akuso governance gates
- do not publish projections without versioning, assumptions, owner review, and approval state
- do not make Akuso AI advantage claims that exceed eval, source, privacy, refusal, incident, or cost evidence
- do not confuse investor interest with product-market proof
