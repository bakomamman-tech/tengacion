# Tengacion Distribution 30/60/90 Day Execution Roadmap

Follow-on roadmap:
[Tengacion Revenue 30/60/90 Day Execution Roadmap](./tengacion-revenue-30-60-90-roadmap.md)

## Purpose

This roadmap is the eighteenth execution cycle after the foundation, operating-system, scale, expansion, platform, ecosystem, network, intelligence, automation, orchestration, resilience, assurance, compliance, audit, certification, institutional, and capital roadmaps. The earlier cycles made Tengacion usable, operable, launchable, expandable, platformized, ecosystem-ready, network-aware, intelligence-led, automation-ready, orchestration-capable, resilience-tested, assurance-backed, compliance-mapped, audit-ready, certification-oriented, institutionally governable, and capital-disciplined. This cycle is about turning capital strategy into controlled distribution:

1. Channel strategy, market focus, and distribution gates
2. Creator supply activation, category programs, and campaign packaging
3. Fan acquisition, retention, referrals, and community loops
4. Sales, partnerships, sponsorships, and revenue operations
5. Brand trust, local market readiness, support capacity, and Akuso go-to-market governance

The goal is not to spend broadly or chase every growth surface. The goal is to decide where Tengacion can earn distribution responsibly, prove that each channel creates creator earnings and repeat fan behavior, and scale only the channels that pass finance, trust, support, compliance, and Akuso quality gates.

## Starting Baseline

This plan assumes the previous roadmap has produced these anchors:

- a capital strategy that explains whether Tengacion should pursue bootstrap discipline, revenue financing, strategic partner funding, grants, venture funding, enterprise prepayments, creator institution partnerships, or delayed fundraising
- a financing readiness scorecard covering traction, revenue quality, creator economics, unit economics, retention, payout confidence, trust controls, market readiness, partner pipeline, security/privacy posture, and Akuso governance
- a financial model with conservative, base, and upside scenarios for GMV, revenue, creator earnings, payouts, refunds, disputes, infrastructure cost, support cost, moderation cost, Akuso cost, partner cost, hiring, runway, and milestone burn
- a capital allocation framework that ties spend to verified learning, creator outcomes, revenue quality, risk reduction, market readiness, and operating leverage
- an investor and strategic partner pipeline segmented by fit, check size, strategic value, diligence burden, control expectations, creator alignment, AI appetite, and market leverage
- diligence-ready packets for investor, strategic partner, enterprise, payment provider, creator institution, market stakeholder, and AI governance conversations
- a capital risk register for dilution, control, revenue quality, creator trust, payout exposure, refund/dispute exposure, market risk, partner concentration, data/security risk, and Akuso cost or safety risk
- governance for investor claims, financial metrics, projections, data-room sharing, diligence Q&A, and external AI claims
- a capital decision on whether to fundraise, pursue strategic capital, pursue revenue-backed growth, delay capital and prove more, or run a targeted partner financing path

The remaining problem is distribution discipline. Tengacion may know what it can fund, but it still needs to know which channels deserve reach, which creator categories can compound, which fan acquisition loops retain, which partner and sales motions produce revenue quality, and which market commitments can be supported without weakening creator trust or operating controls.

## North-star Outcomes For The Next 90 Days

By the end of this plan, Tengacion should have:

- a distribution thesis that ranks channels by creator supply fit, fan demand, CAC confidence, retention potential, revenue quality, support load, trust risk, local market constraints, and Akuso support readiness
- a channel scorecard covering organic creator-led growth, fan referrals, community partnerships, campus or city cohorts, influencer partnerships, paid acquisition, events, strategic partners, enterprise sales, creator institutions, and sponsor-led campaigns
- a creator category activation program for the highest-potential supply areas, with publish targets, offer templates, campaign calendars, quality checks, earnings visibility, support coverage, and churn risk monitoring
- fan acquisition and retention loops that connect first session, first follow, first save, first purchase, first subscription, first referral, and reactivation into measurable cohorts
- a sales and partnerships operating model with approved packages, pricing guardrails, lead stages, claim controls, delivery obligations, reporting cadence, renewal paths, and revocation triggers
- local market readiness gates for launch claims, pricing, payment methods, support tone, moderation load, content rights, regulatory review, partner commitments, and incident response
- brand and trust measurement for public claims, campaign quality, creator trust, support responsiveness, moderation confidence, recommendation complaints, and marketplace safety
- Akuso go-to-market support that helps creators, fans, support, sales, and partners without making unsupported claims, final decisions, or sensitive finance, legal, moderation, account, or market-readiness judgments
- a decision on which distribution channels should scale, which should stay as pilots, which need more evidence, and which should be retired

## Product Principles

- Distribution follows proof: spend and reach should follow creator earnings, fan retention, revenue quality, and trust evidence.
- Creator supply is the first distribution asset: fan demand compounds only when the creator catalog is active, high-quality, and packaged clearly.
- CAC without retention is a warning, not a win: acquisition channels need D7, D30, repeat action, and paid conversion evidence before scale.
- Partner reach needs delivery discipline: sponsorships, institutional partnerships, API access, events, and enterprise commitments require owners, reporting, support, and exit paths.
- Local trust beats generic launch playbooks: pricing, payments, content categories, support language, and creator incentives must fit the market being served.
- Claims stay inside evidence: external growth, finance, AI, creator earnings, market, and partner claims should map to approved metrics and current evidence.
- Akuso supports go-to-market, but does not decide it: Akuso can draft, explain, summarize, and route, while approvals remain in governed workflows.
- Distribution should make the operating system sharper: channels that increase support, payout, moderation, trust, or finance strain without learning should be paused.

## Decision Rules

- If a channel cannot show retention, do not increase spend.
- If creator earnings do not improve, revisit creator supply, packaging, and fan targeting before scaling acquisition.
- If support load rises faster than retained usage, pause or narrow the channel.
- If moderation, rights, refund, dispute, or payout issues rise, pause the affected campaign, category, partner, or market.
- If CAC confidence is weak, keep paid acquisition in learning mode.
- If a partner or sponsor asks for unsupported claims, unsafe data sharing, unlabeled promotion, or unclear delivery obligations, reject or renegotiate.
- If local market readiness is incomplete, do not use broad launch language.
- If finance metrics are estimated or disputed, keep them out of external materials.
- If Akuso unsupported claims increase, pause go-to-market AI expansion and convert misses into evals.
- If the team is overloaded, protect this order:
  - creator supply quality
  - fan retention and purchase trust
  - money movement, refunds, disputes, and payout support
  - support and moderation capacity
  - partner and campaign delivery
  - paid acquisition
  - Akuso distribution expansion
